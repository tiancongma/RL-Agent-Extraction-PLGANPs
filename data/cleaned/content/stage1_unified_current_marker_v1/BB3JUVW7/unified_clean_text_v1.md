Nanocarrier based active targeting strategies against erythrocytic stage of malaria

Nanotechnology has emerged as a promising field for improving conventional therapies by enhancing the bioavailability, pharmacokinetic profile, drug efficacy, reducing the dose, frequency of administration, and toxicity [9]. Several nanocarriers, such as liposomes [10], polymeric nanoparticles [11–15], solid lipid nanoparticles [16], nano lipid carriers [17–19], and ligand-conjugated nanocarriers [20–22], have been reported for delivering antimalarial drugs to target the Plasmodium cycle during the erythrocytic stage. pRBCs have shown structural modifications, including enhanced permeability and overexpression of surface receptors such as glucose transporter 1 (GLUT 1) [23], glucosaminoglycan receptors [24–28], and essential protein receptors [21,29,30].
