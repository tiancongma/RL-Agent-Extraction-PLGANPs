Some hydrophobic natural drugs with high added values are promising in clinical applications, while
their poor water-solubility becomes a major restriction for the bioavailability. To overcome this problem,
the size-reducing strategy is a favorable approach. Herein, model paclitaxel (PTX)@poly (lactide-coglycolic acid) (PLGA) microcapsules are fabricated by an intensified supercritical extraction of emulsion
(SFEE) method. The phase behaviors of carbon dioxide (CO2)/emulsion system during extraction are
investigated to interpret the effects of operation parameters on the extraction. Besides, the emulsion formulas on microcapsule properties are examined experimentally as well. The results indicate that supercritical parameters above the mixture critical point (> 8 MPa at 313 K, for example) enables the great
phase mixing of oil phase and CO2, thus the effectiveness of emulsion extraction can be guaranteed.
Besides, the PLGA mass concentration (cPLGA) and water/oil ratio variations are also proved to favorably
adjust the size, encapsulation efficiency, and drug loading of microcapsules.

2.3. SFEE processing

Fig. 1, there are first-stage and second-stage extractions for the
emulsion in the proposed SFEE process. The CO2 is pressurized by
a high-pressure diaphragm compressor (constant flow rate of 15/
min) and pre-heated in a thermostat water bath, it is then introduced into the high-pressure vessel (inner volume of 500 mL) via
an aeration dispenser at the bottom of vessel cavity. For the firststage emulsion extraction, the vessel is already filled with SCCO2
with desired temperature and pressure, and then the prepared
fresh emulsion is fed by a constant flux pump at the flow rate of
10 mL/min, it is then introduced in the vessel by a customized nozzle, which is installed at upper part of vessel cavity and has an
aperture of 0.5 mm. The emulsion is then fragmentated to form
droplets, and the SCCO2 rapidly diffuses into the oil phase, causing
that the dissolved PTX and PLGA can be precipitated. For the
second-stage emulsion extraction, the pre-extracted emulsion is
accumulated at the bottom of vessel cavity, the SCCO2 aeration
generates numerous bubbles to establish a uniform extraction
field, and the residual solvent in the emulsion can be further
removed. To guarantee the sustained flow of emulsions, the magnetic stirring is equipped for the vessel, and the stirring speed is
set to 600 rpm. As the aimed pressure and temperature are
reached, the exhaust valve opening is regulated to maintain the
pressure dynamic stability in the vessel.

Inspired by the above discussions, a novel SFEE method is proposed to fabricate the model PTX@poly (lactide-co-glycolic acid)
(PLGA) microcapsules in ethyl acetate (EA)/water emulsion. The
PLGA is selected as the polymer matrix, since it is biodegradable
and non-toxic, and has high safety profile . These features
make PLGA a more promising candidate as a carrier for encapsulated drugs. The emulsion injection and SCCO2 aeration are conducted in the SFEE method, aiming to intensify the mass transfer
between SCCO2 and oil phase. The emulsion injection enables the
bulk emulsion fragmentation into droplets in the first-stage extraction, the SCCO2 can rapidly diffuses into the oil phase, leading to
the microcapsule precipitation. For the second-stage extraction,
the SCCO2 aeration can further remove the residual EA in emulsions. The phase behaviors of emulsion/CO2 system are experimentally observed, and the thermodynamic phase equilibrium results
are also presented to interpret the observations and assist in optimizing supercritical parameters. Besides, the emulsion formulas in
terms of mass concentrations of PLGA (cPLGA) and water/oil ratios
on microcapsule properties are also examined. This study confirms

applicability and controllability in the preparation of hydrophobic
drug microcapsules.

As EA is continuously extracted and removed from emulsions
by SCCO2, the PTX@PLGA microcapsules are solidified and then
suspended in the aqueous phase. The whole procedure for all
experiment runs is lasted for 30 min. The PTX@PLGA microcapsules are separated from the obtained suspension and dried overnight, the suspensions and dried solid samples are retained to be
further characterized.

upon the high-pressure phase equilibrium relationships .
The pressure enhancement can significantly improve the solubilities of CO2 in EA, while the temperature exhibits much weaker regulation effects. As the supercritical conditions stay beyond the
mixture critical points (MCP) of EA/CO2 system, the EA and CO2
can achieve the miscibility theoretically, i.e., the extraction effect
of SCCO2 on the oil phase of emulsion is optimized. The valves of
MCP of EA/CO2 system at 313 K and 323 K are ~ 8 MPa and

strengthened along with the increasing temperature or descending
pressure , when the dissolution of CO2 and EA can be accelerated. The above discussions are capable to interpret the results of
aforementioned phase behavior evolutions.
