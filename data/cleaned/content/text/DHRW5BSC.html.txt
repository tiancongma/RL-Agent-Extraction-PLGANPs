Practical quality attributes of polymeric microparticles with current understanding and future perspectives
Furthermore, addition of electrolytes and non-electrolytes in the continuous phase could increase the LC and the EE. Previous studies found that addition of a water-miscible co-solvent such as ethanol, methanol, benzyl alcohol, isopropyl alcohol, dimethyl sulfoxide, or dimethylformamide improved LC of hydrophilic drugs because it enhanced the precipitation rate of the polymer [53–56]. The inclusion of salts such as sodium chloride, sodium bromide, sodium perchlorate, and sodium sulfate also affected LC and EE, as salts influence osmotic pressure difference between the drug trapped within microparticles and continuous phase by limiting escape of the drug from microparticles into continuous phase [54].
