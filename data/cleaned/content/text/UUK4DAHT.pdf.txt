Abstract
To enhance Mangiferin (MNG) the brain bioavailability by developed novel poly-
sorbate-80 (P 80) MNG PLGA NPs and examined the quantity of MNG through
developed a novel method of LC–MS/MS in the ischemic rat brain treatment. The
solvent evaporation method used to develop a novel MNG loaded PLGA NPs fol-
lowed by their coating from polysorbate-80. Polysorbate-80 (P 80) MNG PLGA
NPs developed based on EE, particle size, Zeta Potential, PDI, and loading capacity
with their characterization followed by drug release and intranasal permeation to
enhance the brain bioavailability and also determined their neurobehavioral as well
as biochemical evaluation with the histopathological examination. P 80 MNG PLGA
NPs were optimized with their particle size 103.4 ± 2.66 nm, PDI: 0.201 ± 0.008,
Zeta Potential (−35.8 ± 2.48), and drug loading 37.16 ± 2.09% with 76.08 ± 4.91%

Introduction
As per WHO reports, the ischemic brain stroke is a third largest problem related
to health that is responsible for disability or death. Free radicals production is the
main cause after the cerebral ischemia, which can be protect by the use of natural
antioxidant. The medicinal plants and plant foods are the major source of antioxi-
dants that can be easily scavenge the free radicals [1]. The ischemic brain stroke has
affected near about 15 million peoples all over the world. The 33.30% peoples were
died every year and rest of the 50% peoples affected from permanent disabilities.
This is a very big problem for the community. Ischemia brain stroke covers ~ 87.0%
of the cases due to the cause of interruption of blood flow & ­O2 in the arteries of
brain. Therefore, it also damages the brain physiological activity, which leads to a
complex pathophysiological incident followed by the involvement of the interaction
in-between the both systems, i.e., nervous & immune system. The main mechanism
for pathobiology of ischemia or reperfusion (IR) injury contains oxidative stress,
excitotoxicity, inflammation, & apoptosis [2].
It was reported before that based on their evidences related to oxidative stress.
The oxidative stress is a major role to damage the brain due to creating the obstruc-
tion and reperfusion finally resulting stroke. The reactive oxygen species (ROS) is
the main cause which is associated with the pathogenesis of ischemia reperfusion

Mangiferin, i.e., chemical name 1,3,6,7-tetrahydroxyxanthone C2-β-D-glucoside

enging of free radical due to their C-glucosyl linkage & polyhydroxy component
[11, 12]. MNG was used in the suppression of MAO-B of brain in animal models of
Parkinson’s disease [13, 14]. Mangiferin enhanced the levels of glutathione that also
decreases the levels of ROS and malondialdehyde (MDA) that showing MNG quan-

for drug delivery [21], carbon nanotubes used after PEGylation [20], some hydro-
gels used as injectable [21], nano micelles [22] and lipid based nanocarriers [23]
were previously prepared by so many scientists for the delivery of proposed drug
through different routes of administrations.
MNG showed maximum lipophilicity, very small solubility, very less permeabil-
ity of intestinal in biological fluids with very little bioavailability followed by quick
elimination from the body without any significant clinical results. There is a need to
develop any type of dosage form to limit this prospect [18, 19]. BBB allows cross-

be targeted to the brain with the help of Targeted Drug Delivery System (TDDS)
and it can easily cross their protective covering. These barriers can create an obsta-
cle to treat the diseases of brain, decreasing the ability of drug delivery systems
(DDS). Therefore, there is a need of TDDS which will transport the drug by the
BBB without any decreasing its efficiency [24, 25]. Now a days, the most important
use of nano materials are delivered to the brain via crossing the BBB that contains
a characteristic feature like PLGA nanoparticles which is safe and biodegradable
after hydrolyzation gives glycolic and lactic acid as a safe endogenous metabolite

Materials and methods
We buy Mangiferin (MNG), PLGA, and Chitosan from Sigma, USA. We used
LC–MS grade solvents like Ammonium formate, Acetonitrile, ammonium acetate,
Methanol, formic acid buys from Sigma-Aldrich Corporation, USA and others
chemicals are analytical grade. Milli Q Water was used also in the required places of
conducted this research.
Preparation of P 80 MNG PLGA NPs
The nanoparticles prepared based on PLGA polymer via emulsification solvent
evaporation method and load the MNG with little bit modification of the method
[17, 47–49]. We dissolved the PLGA first in 3.0% dichloromethane (DCM) after
that addition of 0.6% w/v MNG in ethanol. The above mixture was sonicated in the
ice after their emulsification. 0.30% Vitamin E TPGS solution (10 mL) was added
slowly-slowly, i.e., via drop in the PLGA mixture. It has given maximum emulsi-
fication at 0 °C via sonicator. Magnetic-stirring was applied on the above mixture
formulation at whole night to remove DCM via evaporation. The PLGA mixture
centrifuged at 18,000 rpm for 1 h on the 2nd day followed by washing 3 times for
the removal of unloaded MNG. Finally, the precipitate dissolved and re-suspended
in Milli-Q-water. The obtained formulation was freeze dried (Lab Conco., LPYH,
Lock 6, USA freeze dryer) that gives the pellets and after that it converted into pow-
der form as MNG-loaded PLGA NPs. At the end, optimized MNG PLGA NPs were
coated by polysorbate-80 (P-80; 1.5%) which was stirring continuously upto 1 h.
Finally, the P 80 MNG PLGA NPs was prepared.

Neurobehavioral study
Study of rota‑rod
Apparatus for Rota-rod (Omnitech, USA) was taken to determine the coordination of
rat’s muscles after 22 h of reperfusion reported before [54]. All rats have trained before
6 days to initiate our research study. We have taken 4 animals at a time to perform the

time of rats from rotating shaft via omni-rotor. The cutoff time was 180 sec at 10 c/
min for the speed of rotating-shaft. We have determined the latency time or rotating rod
time which was prior fixed by each rat’s staying time.
Examination of activity of spontaneous motor (SMA)
The test was performed for the SMA examination based on Ahmad et al. examination
after 24 h of reperfusion [55]. The animals were kept into their cages as described in
groups for 5 min to evaluate the SMA. Scores for Neurological assessment were con-
sidered as Freely move rats based on free atmosphere independently = 0; 1: There is a
hesitation for cages to touch their walls but not touch; 2 Score: there is an indication of
postural defectiveness curved toward paretic side due to not moving properly or very
bad movement; and 3 Score: There is no movements of rats observed which is clear
indication of postural defectiveness curved toward paretic side.
FT (Flexion test)
Vaibhav et al. method was used to perform the FT test [56]. Based on their scoring, the
scaling is 0: There is no deficit of neurologically; Score 1: Adduction of shoulder and
wrist flexion that means contralateral forelimb flexion; There is a decrement in lateral
push resistance = 2; There is movement inside the paretic side of circle = 3.
Grip strength examination
The previous reported method followed for the examination of Grip strength [55]. We
tied a long string (50.0 cm) in between two perpendicular supports. The scale was
selected and evaluated on these bases: 0 (Zero) = rat fell off; 1 (One) = hanging of rats
continuously with two fore paws on string; Two (2): There is no difference in between
but animals tried to climb on string; Animals were hanged with both front paws on
string but one or both limbs are hind = 3; Four (4): Tails were wrapped on the string
and also hanged with the help of their two front paws; and Five (5) = escaped the rats.

Evaluation of biochemical studies
Preparation of tissue
The brain was removed after the sacrifice followed by cutting to separate the cortex
area. We homogenized the brain-tissues [5% w/v] in the PBS [10.0 mmol/l, 7.4 pH)
and centrifuged (800 g for 5 min at 4.0 °C) to take supernatant as S1. This superna-
tant (S1) was used for the assay of thiobarbituric acid reactive substance (TBARS)
and remaining S1 was re-centrifuged at 10,500 × g for 15 min at 4 °C (S2) to sep-
arate post-mitochondrial supernatant (PMS) which was used for the estimation of
antioxidant enzymes.
Examination of LPO
Assay of TBARS was performed to examine the LPO via their byproduct of lipid
peroxidation. The previous reported method was used for the TBARS assay [55].
The homogenate (100 µL) was taken followed by incubation at 37 °C in a meta-
bolic water bath shaker. Rest 100 µL of same homogenate was incubated at 0.0 °C.
TCA (10%, 500  µL) and TBA TCA (0.67%, 500  µL) were transferred into both
homogenate samples after finishing incubation and then centrifuged both samples
again at 3000 × g for 15 min. The supernatant taken for both and transferred into
two fresh tubes followed by keeping on boiling water bath for 10 min. All the tubes
were cool down at normal temperature followed by analyzed the samples at 535 nm.
LPO rates were calculated the in μ mole of TBARS formed ­hr−1 ­gm−1 tissue using
1.56 × ­105 ­M−1 ­cm−1 molar extinction coefficient.

Conclusion
The solvent evaporation method was used for the preparation of MNG loaded PLGA
NPs in which MNG was entrapped inside PLGA NPs. MNG PLGA NPs and P 80
MNG PLGA NPs were optimized and characterized by DL, EE, and their size of
the particles. A novel LC–MS/MS method was successfully developed which was
used to examine the PK-parameters of P 80 MNG PLGA NPs. P 80 MNG PLGA
NPs showed the controlled and sustained release of MNG upto 24 h. MNG (P 80
MNG PLGA) was targeted to the brain through i.n. route escaping the first pass
hepatic metabolism. The brain bioavailability of MNG was improved from P 80
MNG PLGA NPs at a very small dose, which directly entered into the brain via i.n.
route as compared to MNG simple suspension (i.v.). P 80 MNG PLGA NPs shows
a significant result from the MCAO Longa et al. model of ischemic brain for the
examination of neurobehavioral, biochemical analysis, and histopathological analy-
sis. P 80 MNG PLGA NPs were found to be used safely and effectively by i.n. route
to brain drug delivery in the treatment of cerebral ischemia. Toxicity results has
confirmed that our developed and optimized P 80 MNG PLGA NPs are safe. Poly-
sorbate-80 can promote MNG distribution in the brain by increasing MNG systemic
absorption. It also improves the passive transport of MNG via BBB from nose-to-
brain delivery. In future, P 80 MNG PLGA NPs can be used in the ischemic brain
treatment safely. It can be used as an effective way of MNG delivery to brain with a
novel noninvasive route but required clinical study on a large scale.
