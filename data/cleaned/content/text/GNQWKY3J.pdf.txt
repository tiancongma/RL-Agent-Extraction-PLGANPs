EpCAM aptamer activated 5-FU-loaded PLGA nanoparticles in CRC
treatment; in vitro and in vivo study

journal contribution
posted on 2022-11-28, 05:20 authored by Bahram Yavari, Seyyed
Shamsadin Athari, Yadollah Omidi, Akram Jalali, Rezvan Najafi
<p>In this study, epithelial cell adhesion molecule (EpCAM) aptamer-
activated nanoparticles (Ap-NPs) were synthesised to enhance treatment
efficiency in colorectal cancer (CRC). PLGA [poly(d, l-lactide-<i>co</
i>-glycolide)] copolymer was fabricated by conjugation of COOH-PEG-
NH<sub>2</sub> to PLGA-COOH through an EDC/NHS-mediated
chemistry. Afterwards, 5-fluorouracil-loaded (FU) nanoparticles were
prepared using the water/oil/water double emulsion solvent
evaporation method. The <i>in vitro</i> cytotoxicity of
formulations was evaluated using the MTT assay in HCT-116, CT-26 and
HEK-293 cell lines. For <i>in vivo</i> study, tumour-bearing BALB/
c mice were established by subcutaneous injection of CT-26 cell line.
The results indicated that fabricated AP-FU-NPs had 101 nm size with a
spherical surface, relatively homogeneously and, satisfactory
encapsulation efficiency (83.93%). <i>In vitro</i> experiments
revealed that Ap-FU-NPs had a superior <i>in vitro</i> cytotoxicity
than both FU-NPs and free 5-FU in CT-26 and HCT-116 cells but, were
significantly low toxic against HEK-293 cells relative to free 5-FU.
Furthermore, <i>in vivo</i> results showed no significant
haemolytic effect, hepatic and renal injury, or weight loss. After
treatment of various animal groups with formulations, notable tumour
growth delay was observed following the order: Ap-FU-NPs < FU-NPs <
5-FU < PBS. The results suggest that AP-FU-NPs could be an effective
and promising carrier for 5-FU delivery to the EpCAM overexpressing

CRC cells.</p> <p>The present study was carried out in the three
steps summarised: (1) synthesis and characterisation. At first, PEG was
conjugated to PLGA polymers by EDC/NHS chemistry method, then the
resulting polymers were used to prepare PLGA-PEG NPs <i>via</i>
the W/O/W technique. Finally, EpCAM Aptamers were attached to
fabricated NPs. (2) In the second step cytotoxicity of formulated NPs
was evaluated <i>in vitro</i> experiments in HEK-293, CT-26 and
HCT-116 cell lines. (3) In the third step <i>in vitro</i> results
further was investigated in CT-26 tumour-bearing BALB/c mice. ‘Created
with BioRender.com’.</p>
