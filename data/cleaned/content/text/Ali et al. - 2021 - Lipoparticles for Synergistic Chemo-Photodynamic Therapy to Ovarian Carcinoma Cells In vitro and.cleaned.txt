Temoporfin (Meso-tetrahyroxyphenyl chlorine; mTHPC)
is one of the most potent second-generation photosensitizers
used for the treatment of a variety of clinical disorders. After
being administered, the mTHPC can be preferentially loca
lized into the tumor tissue by exploiting the tumor's EPR
(enhanced permeability and retention) effect. After being
activated by a specific wavelength (652 nm), it produces
free radicals and singlet oxygen species that caused the
destruction of cancer cells.7 However, its hydrophobic nat
ure, poor aqueous solubility, and low bioavailability limit its
application in anticancer therapies.8 To overcome these
potential issues, the liposomal formulation of mTHPC was
developed where it is encapsulated in a hydrophobic milieu
thus increasing the bioavailability of the drug.
