Particulate formulations for the delivery of poly(I: C) as vaccine adjuvant
Three processes were commonly used for the preparation of antigen-loaded PLGA microspheres: (i) phase separation, frequently also called coacervation [158], (ii) spray-drying [158,159] and (iii) solvent extraction/evaporation [160]. To enable an aseptic and large scale continuous production of PLGA microspheres, a microextrusion-based solvent extraction process using a static micromixer was developed [160–162]. This technique presents the advantage of permitting concomitant formation and surface functionalization of anionic PLGA microspheres with cationic polymers in a one-step process [137,150].
