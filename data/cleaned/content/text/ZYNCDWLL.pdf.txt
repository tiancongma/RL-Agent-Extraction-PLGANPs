Abstract
Rheumatoid arthritis, an autoimmune disorder, has increased globally due to dysfunctioning of
rheumatoid factor and anti-citrullinated protein antibody. The clinical manifestation includes
progressive disability of synovial joints due to inﬂammation of synovial membrane. The outlined
research work was carried out with an aim to prepare microneedle arrays to co-administer folic acid
with methotrexate for transepidermal delivery in the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis. The
microspheres of poly (lactic-co-glycolic acid) containing folic acid were developed by using multiple
emulsion (w/o/w type) system. Polyvinylpyrrolidone-based microneedles arrays of methotrexate
were casted by using silicon based moulds. Microspheres were analysed for particle size, entrapment
efﬁciency and in-vitro release of folic acid studies. Scanning electron microscopy showed that the
microsphere exhibits sphere-like structure within narrow size range of 7±1.1 to 13±1.3 μm.
In-vitro release study showed controlled release of drugs from microneedle arrays up to 24 h.
Pharmacodynamics efﬁcacy study of microneedle array was estimated by using Complete Freund’s
Adjuvant (CFA) model in female Albino Wistar rats and showed suppression of paw swelling. It was
concluded that the transepidermal microneedle technology for co-administration of folic acid with
methotrexate for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis is a minimally invasive, cost-effective and an
alternative to the conventional drug delivery system.
1. Introduction
Inﬂammatory arthritis is a cluster of diseases which
includes several acute and chronic inﬂammatory
conditions of synovial joints like rheumatoid arthritis
(RA). The prevalence of RA is estimated to be 25–30
per 100, 000 cases and females are more susceptible
than males. RA, an auto-immuno disorder, is a slow
degenerative disease characterized by joint swelling,
tenderness and inﬂammation of synovial joints with
cartilage and bone destruction. The rheumatoid factor
and anti-citrullinated protein antibody are involved in
RA but exact pathophysiological reasons yet to be
cleare [1–6]. The treatment of RA is presently available
in combinations of NSAIDs, Disease Modifying Anti
Rheumatoid Drugs DMARDs, and glucocorticoids
[7]. Unlike NSAIDs and glucocorticoids, DMARDs are
believed to alter RA progression, by reducing and
preventing joint destruction [8] Although it is difﬁcult
to predict the effectiveness of DMARDs agent on the
patients but 2/3rd of the patients have shown positive
clinical manifestations. The DMARDs agents like
sulfasalazine, hydroxychloroquine, methotrexate and
gold conjugates are most commonly used in ﬁrst-line
therapy. Since two decades, DMARD’s are obvious for
providing rapid onset of action, to increase efﬁcacy, to
reduce toxicity and ease of administration to patients.
The antiarthritic mechanism of methotrexate is not
very clear but is believed to have anti-metabolite
activity by inhibiting purine synthesis [9, 10]. The use
of microneedle technology achieves painless and
targeted delivery for vaccines, drugs and proteins. The
co-administration of folic acid with methotrexate is to
combat the depleting folate by reserving the inhibitory

2. Materials and methods
2.1. Materials
Poly lactic-co-glycolic acid (PLGA; lactide/glycolide
molar ratio of 75:25) was procured from Evonik,
Mumbai, India. Polyvinyl alcohol (PVA; MW 1, 25,
000) was obtained from CDH Laboratory New Delhi,
India. Polyvinylpyrrolidone (PVP; MW 10, 000) was
obtained from Sigma Aldrich, USA. Sodium bicarbo-
nate (NaHCO3) and dichloromethane (DCM) were
procured from Spectrochem, Mumbai, India. Com-
plete Freund’s Adjuvant (CFA) was purchased from
Sigma Aldrich, USA. Methotrexate was obtained as a
gift sample by Khandelwal Laboratories, Mumbai,
India, Folic acid was procured from Loba Chemie,
Mumbai, India. All other chemicals and reagents used
were of analytical grade.
3. Fabrication of PLGA microspheres
containing folic acid
The PLGA microspheres were formulated using multi-
ple emulsion (water-in-oil-in-water) approach by
using solvent-diffusion-evaporation method [13]. The
process involved the following sequential events:
ﬁrstly the primary emulsion was prepared by emulsify-
ing aqueous solution of PVA in 1 ml NaHCO3 and
folic acid with PLGA solution in 2 ml of DCM. The
emulsiﬁcation was carried out using magnetic stirrer
(Remi, India) at 1300 rpm for 10 min. Then, this
preformed primary emulsion was added to 6 ml of
PVA solution and was stirred at 1500 rpm for half an
hour to prepare multiple emulsion (w/o/w). This
emulsion system was then carefully added into 6 ml of
distilled water and was allowed to stir overnight at
ambient temperature to evaporate DCM. The solidi-
ﬁed microspheres were obtained via centrifugation at
8000 rpm for 30 min. Afterwards the microspheres
were rinsed and re-suspended in 10 ml of distilled
water. The composition of different formulations
from FA1 to FA3 is mentioned in table 1.
3.1. Characterization of PLGA microspheres
containing folic acid
3.1.1. Morphology
The surface properties of microsphere formulations
FA1, FA2 and FA3 were evaluated by Scanning electron
microscopy (SEM). The analyte was mounted on the
aluminium stubs using double sided carbon tape. The
microspheres were sprinkled onto the tape. The alumi-
nium stubs were placed in SEM (Philips, Netherlands)
inside vacuum chamber. The microspheres were
observed for morphological characterization using a
gaseous secondary electron detector (working pressure:
0.8 Torr, and acceleration voltage: 30.00 kV) XL 30,
Philips(Eindhoven,The Netherlands).
3.1.2. Entrapment efﬁciency
PLGA microspheres containing folic acid equivalent
to 3.5 mg of folic acid was added in 10 ml of phosphate
buffer (PB) pH 5.5 and sonicated for 30 min at
ambient temperature. The drug content was analyzed
by UV–vis spectrophotometer (Shimadzu, USA) at
256 nm. The drug encapsulation efﬁciency was deter-
mined by the following formula: [14]

3.1.3. In-vitro drug release study
In-vitro release study of PLGA microsphere formula-
tions (FA1, FA2 and FA3) containing folic acid was
performed by using dialysis membrane method in PB
pH 5.5 at 37±0.5 °C. Folic acid microspheres were
dispersed in 2 ml of PB pH 5.5 and then placed into
semipermeable dialysis membrane (Himedia, Mum-
bai, India). This system was immersed in a cylinder
containing 30 ml of PB pH 5.5 subjected to continuous
stirring at 50 rpm at ambient temperature. Aliquots
were withdrawn at different time intervals of 1, 2, 4, 8
and 24 h and the medium was replaced with an
equal amount of the PB pH 5.5 to maintain a sink
condition. The concentration of folic acid was ana-
lyzed using UV–vis spectrophotometer (Shimadzu,
USA) at 256 nm.
On the basis of results of entrapment efﬁciency
and in-vitro drug release studies of microspheres,
formulation FA2 was selected for preparation of
microneedles.

4.1. Characterization of microneedle of
methotrexate containing of PLGA microspheres of
folic acid
4.1.1. Morphology
The surface characteristics of microneedles of metho-
trexate containing PLGA microspheres were studied
by SEM (XL 30 ESEM with EDAX, Philips, Nether-
lands). The samples were observed for morphological
characterization using a gaseous secondary electron
detector at working pressure: 0.8 Torr, acceleration
voltage: 30.00 kV XL 30, Philips (Eindhoven, The
Netherlands).
4.1.2. Drug content
The drug content in the microneedle formulations
(MN1, MN2 and MN3) was estimated by dissolving
the formulation equivalent to their doses of folic acid
and methotrexate in PB pH 5.5. The absorbance was
determined by UV–vis spectrophotometer (Shimadzu,
Japan) to estimate folic acid and methotrexate at
256 nm and 302 nm respectively.
Based on the results of drug content, formulation
MN2 was selected for in vitro release and pharmacody-
namics studies.
4.1.3. In vitro release proﬁle
The microneedle formulations MN2 was attached to
the bottom of a glass plates, which are placed on Franz
diffusion cell those contained 25 ml of PB pH 5.5, with
stirring speed 50 rpm at a temperature 37 °C. Aliquots
were removed at different time intervals of 1, 2, 4, 8
and 24 h and replaced with an equal amount of fresh
PB pH 5.5 to maintain sink condition. The release of
folic acid and methotrexate from microneedle formu-
lation was analyzed using UV–vis spectrophotometer
(Shimadzu, Japan) at wavelengths of 256 nm and
302 nm respectively.
4.1.4. FTIR spectroscopy
FTIR spectra of methotrexate, folic acid, formulation
FA2, microspheres without folic acid, formulation
MN2, microneedle containing methotrexate and
microneedle without drug were recorded in the range
of 400–4000 cm−1 using KBr pellet method using an
FTIR spectrophotometer (Perkin Elmer, USA).
4.1.5. DSC
DSC (Mettler Toledo, Switzerland) analyses of folic
acid, methotrexate, microneedle without drugs and
formulation MN2 were used to study the thermal
behaviour of the microneedles. The experiments were
performed in presence of nitrogen at heating rate of
10 °C min−1.
4.1.6. Pharmacodynamics study
Female Albino Wistar rats weighing (150–160 g) were
divided in 3 different study groups (n=6 each
group). First group served as a control, second group
received only CFA intradermally while third group
received CFA intradermally and PVP microneedle
arrays on the site of CFA injection. On day 0, the paw
volume of each animals was measured followed by
CFA injection (0.1 ml) in right hind paw except
control group which received parafﬁn oil (0.1 ml).
After 7 days, microneedle formulation MN2 that
contained microspheres were inserted into the skin
once in a week for 28 days. Paw volume of all animals
was measured by Digital Plethysmometer (IITC, USA)
on day 0th, 7th, 14th, 21st and 28th day [15].
5. Results and discussion
5.1. PLGA microspheres of folic acid
5.1.1. Morphology
SEM results revealed that the microspheres exhibited
sphere like structures within a narrow size range from
7±1.1 to 13±1.3 μm (Figure 1) which might be
due to polymeric structure of PLGA which maintain
the spherical geometry [16].
5.1.2. % Entrapment efﬁciency
The entrapment efﬁciencies of formulations of FA1,
FA2 and FA3 were found to be 30%–80% as shown in
ﬁgure 2. The study had also revealed that an increase in
entrapment efﬁciency with increase in concentration
of PLGA polymer up to 7.5 mg ml−1 while further
increase in polymer concentration to 10 mg ml−1,
the % entrapment efﬁciency was found to be
decreased. The entrapment efﬁciency of microspheres
using an O/W/O method was dependent on drug
partition coefﬁcient in the internal and external
phases. PVA was added into the internal aqueous
phase to yield a relatively stable primary emulsion and
providing better drugs entrapment [14]. The entrap-
ment efﬁciency was used as a screening factor for
selection of formulations and found that batch FA2
exhibited a maximum entrapment of 80.16±1.76%.
Then it was selected to be incorporated within the
microneedles array.

5.2.3. In-vitro release study
In-vitro release study showed within 8 h more than
80% of methotrexate and nearly 60% of folic acid was
released from the microneedle arrays, which were
followed by progressive controlled release up to 24 h
as shown in ﬁgure 6. PVP is most widely used
biocompatible polymer, as it is an US FDA-approved.
Various grades of PVP can be used, depending on the
strength and brittleness required. It is also widely
used as a blood plasma expander and in pharmaceu-
tical industry for various purposes. It is highly
suitable in formulating transdermal delivery systems,
and thus becomes an apt choice for microneedle
formulations [17]. The use of water-soluble polymer
like PVP eliminates the risk of biohazardous sharp
waste deposition in the skin. Drug release from the
system is depending on the hydrophilicity of the
polymer. The hydrophilicity of polymer causes ero-
sion of system which leads to drug release by
formation of small channels into the matrix from
which the solvent migrates in and dissolves the
drug and it comes out through the channels. The
hydrophobicity of polymer causes erosion of system
which causes drug release [18, 19].
