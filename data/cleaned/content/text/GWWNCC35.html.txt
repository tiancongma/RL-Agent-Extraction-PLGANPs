Bioavailability and biodistribution of nanodelivered lutein
Nevertheless, the amount of PLGA-NP translocated into Caco-2 cell monolayers appears minimal because of low endocytic capacity (Cartiera, Johnson, Rajendran, Caplan, & Saltzman, 2009;). The better uptake and secretion of lutein when micelle components were added to PLGA-NP lutein is consistent with the notion that 30% of hydrophobic compounds are released from the NP (Derakhshandeh et al., 2011; Gang et al., 2013), which are readily available for assembly into micelles for uptake and into chylomicrons for secretion (Luchoomun & Hussain, 1999). Thus, our data suggests that the bioavailability of nanodelivered hydrophobic compounds will be improved when combined with micelle components or fats.
