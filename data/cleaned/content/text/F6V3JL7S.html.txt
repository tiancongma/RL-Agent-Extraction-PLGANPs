Materials Science and Engineering: C
Biodegradable poly(D,L-lactide- co -glycolide)/poly(L-γ-glutamic acid) nanoparticles conjugated to folic acid for targeted delivery of doxorubicin
Highlights
• A targeted drug carrier based on PLGA/γ-PGA nanoparticles conjugated to folic acid is proposed.
• FA conjugation by carbodiimide provides a suitable method to obtain multimeric and multivalent nanoparticles.
• Doxorubicin release from sensitive and responsive nanoparticles with pH and time exposure dependence was observed.
• Polymeric nanoparticles uptake was enhanced by a receptor-mediated mechanism.

Synthesis and evaluation of polyethylene glycol- and folic acid-conjugated polyamidoamine G4 dendrimer as nanocarrier
Conjugation of dendrimers with folic-acid (441 Da) biocompatible agent for selective delivery of anti-cancer bioactives to tumour is one of the most important approaches in targeted drug delivery systems (TDDS). The folic acid receptor (FR) as a kind of 38-kDa glycosyl phosphotidylinositol-anchored protein is the first one to be over expressed in many types of tumour cells including those of the breast, ovaries, lung, kidney, colorectal, and brain, while its expression is limited in normal tissues and organs [19–23]. Folic acid (FA) has been known to target FA receptors (FAR) that are overexpressed in many human carcinomas including colorectal, breast, etc. [12–14]. The high expression of FAR on cancer cells represents a great potential in achieving selective tumor targeting in a large number of cancers [15–20]. On the other hand, polyethelen glycation (PEG) of polymeric nanoparticles is frequently applied to increase stability, and thus the half-life, as well as the non-immunogenic and non-antigenic properties [21–23].
