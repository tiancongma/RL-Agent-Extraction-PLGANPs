## Abstract
Dysregulation of pro-inflammatory cytokines participates in the initiation and development of knee osteoarthritis (OA).
Consequently, interventions to boost the anti-inflammatory capacity of articular chondrocytes have been proposed to treat earlystage OA and prevent OA progression. Applying nanoencapsulation can enhance bioavailability and bioactivity and sustain
the anti-inflammatory activity of phytochemicals. Accordingly, in this study, we used nanocapsules to deliver curcumin (Cur)
to treat inflammatory chondrocytes. Using double-emulsion technology, Cur was encapsulated in chitosan-coated poly(lacticco-glycolic acid) nanocapsules. The nanocapsulized Cur (NCcur) was characterized, and the toxicity to human articular chondrocytes was evaluated. NCcur was applied to interleukin-1 beta (IL-1b)-stimulated cells based on findings of the Cur toxicity
study. Results showed that the particle size of NCcur was 247.8 +- 1.73 nm with a zeta potential of 20.3 +- 0.11 mV and a mid-range
distribution. NCcur showed a core-shell and sphere-like morphology. The encapsulation efficiency of Cur in nanocapsules was
67.1%. Nanoencapsulation decreased the toxicity of high-dose Cur (> 20 mM), and NCcur exhibited a sustained Cur release over
72 h. NCcur supplementation (20 mM) improved cell survival and ameliorated cell senescence of inflammatory chondrocytes.
The IL-1b-induced IL1B, IL6, metalloproteinase-9 (MMP9), and MMP13 mRNA expressions were down-regulated, while IL10
level was enhanced in NCcur-treated chondrocytes. Likewise, NCcur supplementation restored aggrecan, collagen type II alpha
1 chain, and SOX9 mRNA expressions. MMP-13, IL-8, and MCP-1 secretions in the supernatant also decreased. By applying nanocapsules, we assumed the anti-inflammatory capacity of Cur could be sustained for treating knee OA.

Curcumin (Cur), a turmeric-derived polyphenol, is a versatile
phytochemical isolated from Curcuma species. The chemical
and structural characteristics of Cur are crucial for its biological activities . It is a diferuloylmethane molecule, known
structurally as 1,7-bis(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)-1,6-hept
adiene-3,5-dione. Cur exhibits a range of bioactive functions,
including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, anticancer, and immunomodulatory properties . Cur especially showed promise
in OA treatment by suppressing inflammatory mediators of OA
chondrocytes in vitro . Moreover, Cur was shown to slow OA
progression, relieve pain scores, and modulate extracellular matrix (ECM) homeostasis in various animal models [7, 8]. In addition, oral administration of Cur is a recommended adjunctive
treatment for knee OA due to pain relief effects . Despite Cur
showing solid evidence of chondroprotective ability, it is also
known that the bioavailability of orally administered Cur is low,
and the absorption of Cur results in rapid loss in the gastrointestinal tract .

Applying nanotechnologies can improve the bioavailability
and bioactivity and sustain the antioxidant activities of phytochemicals . Advances in nanoparticle fabrication have also
benefited Cur-based complexes as effective therapeutic agents
. Nanoparticles can be classified into nanospheres and nanocapsules based on their structural organization. A nanocapsule
is a core-shell structure with active agents entrapped within
the core. The polymeric wall commonly consists of biodegradable materials that are important in controlling drug release.
Advantages of using nanocapsules as a drug delivery system
include a low burst effect, low drug irritation, and a high loading capacity [13, 14]. Poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid) (PLGA), a biodegradable copolymer, has been approved by the US Food and
Drug Administration for drug delivery systems. In the human
body, hydrolysis of PLGA produces lactic acid and glycolic acid,
which can easily be metabolized through the citric acid cycle
[15, 16]. In addition to drug delivery, PLGA has been widely used
as a cell carrier for cartilage repair . Chitosan is a biodegradable polysaccharide consisting of b-(1-4)-linked d-glucosamine
and N-acetyl-d-glucosamine. The structure of chitosan is similar to native polysaccharides comprising articular cartilage
. Several studies showed that chitosan can promote cartilage
tissue formation by increasing glycosaminoglycan (GAG) and
Type II collagen secretion by chondrocytes . Accordingly,
chitosan-coated PLGA nanocapsules were fabricated as a drug
delivery carrier of Cur. Therapeutic potentials of nanocapsulized Cur (NCcur) toward human OA chondrocytes were evaluated in this study.

The IL-1b pro-inflammatory cytokine is known as a critical
factor that contributes to OA progression, and thus, IL-1bstimulated articular chondrocytes are a well-accepted in vitro
OA cell model . Both Cur and NCcur supplementations
ameliorated IL-1b-induced cell death (Figure 3b), while the
mitochondrial activity of chondrocytes did not fully recover
(Figure 3a). Chen et al. pretreated rat chondrocytes with Cur
and then exposed cells to IL-1b . They reported that Cur
pre-treatment ameliorated IL-1b-induced apoptosis, while cell
death (LDH release level) was still higher than in normal cells.
In addition, IL-1b is also known to cause cell apoptosis and senescence . Our results revealed that NCcur supplementation
relieved cell death (Figure 4b) and cell senescence (Figure 4d)
of IL-1b-stimulated chondrocytes. Similarly, Deng et al. noted
that 10 mM Cur could alleviate senescence of bone marrow mesenchymal stem cells in vitro . The molecular mechanism by
which NCcur affects cell survival and senescence should be further validated.

While NCcur shows chondroprotective effects on IL-1bstimulated human articular chondrocytes, nanoencapsulation
may change the biological activity of Cur. When compared to
the free form, the bioaccessibility and bioactivity of Cur decreased after encapsulation . Therefore, the therapeutic
potentials of Cur and NCcur shall be compared. Additionally,
intra-articular administration is a proposed route for delivering
NCcur. A knee OA animal model should be used to assess the
retention of NCcur in the damaged cartilage and its in vivo therapeutic effects in the future.

## Discussion
Applying phytochemicals was proposed as a way to modulate inflammatory cascades in the pathological progression of OA .
Despite recognized chondroprotective effects, Cur is known to
be rapidly degraded in physiological conditions and has a low
bioavailability. On the contrary, Cur packed in nanocapsules
showed superior anti-edematogenic activity without impairing
normal hepatic functions in an animal model of arthritis . In
addition, Cur-loaded nanocapsules were also found to exert an
antioxidant capacity and promote M2 macrophage polarization
. Therefore, delivering NCcur could be a promising approach
for treating knee OA.

## Conclusion
Chitosan-coated PLGA NCcur was fabricated using a doubleemulsion method with a core-shell and sphere-like structure,
a particle size of 247.8 +- 1.73 nm, and a positive zeta potential
of 20.3 +- 0.11 mV. Nanoencapsulation decreased the high-dose
toxicity of Cur, and NCcur showed a sustained Cur release.
NCcur supplementation enhanced cell survival and alleviated
cell senescence of IL-1b-treated chondrocytes. IL-1b-induced
IL1B, IL6, MMP9, and MMP13 mRNA expressions were downregulated, while IL10, ACAN, COL2A1, and SOX9 levels were
increased in NCcur-treated chondrocytes. In addition, NCcur
also decreased MMP-13, IL-8, and MCP-1 secretions, while IL1b and IL-6 productions did not decrease. NCcur may improve
homeostasis of the knee joint and has the potential to be applied
as OA therapy.

NCcur supplementation decreased IL-lb-induced IL1B, IL6,
MMP9, and MMP13 mRNA levels and restored reductions in
ACAN and COL2A1 expressions (Figure 5), which was similar to the biological actions of Cur on chondrocytes . These
findings reveal that the nanocapsules did not influence the bioactive functions of Cur, and NCcur could regulate inflammatory reactions and modulate ECM-related genes in particular
chondrocytes. Moreover, NCcur enhanced mRNA expression
of the IL10 anti-inflammatory cytokine by IL-1b-treated chondrocytes. D'Angeli et al. also reported that poly(e-caprolactone)
NCcur down-regulated IL1B and up-regulated IL10 mRNA levels in lipopolysaccharide-treated adipose-derived stem cells .
Furthermore, the chondrogenic marker SOX9, vital in maintaining the functional phenotype of chondrocytes, was restored in
NCcur-treated inflammatory chondrocytes. Wang et al. showed
similar findings of Cur up-regulating SOX9 mRNA expression
by IL-1b-stimulated mouse knee chondrocytes . Therefore,
NCcur can modulate inflammatory mediators and enhance
chondrogenic and ECM-related gene expressions, which are important to cartilage homeostasis.
