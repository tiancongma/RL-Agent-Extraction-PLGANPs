It is reasonable to expect that any condition leading to a
decrease in hyperuricemia in synovial fluid will promote dissolution of deposited crystals and also inhibit formation of
other crystals. This is achieved primarily by using uratelowering therapy (uricase). MSU crystals disappear from the

to shift in the crystallization equilibrium to backward direction. Uricase has low activation energy, high substrate enzyme
stabilization energy, and also requires lower Gibb's free energy to form transition state due to which it releases high amount

high catalytic efficiency, due to transition state stabilization,
and efficiency in the degradation of uric acid and urate crystals

the use of different polymers and solvents. Optimization was
done by Taguchi orthogonal (L-4) design in which, most of
the time, responses are affected by a small number of main
effects and lower order interactions while higher order interactions are relatively unimportant. Nanoparticles possess a
characteristic particle size and size distribution which are considered as most important characteristics for drug release. A
smaller size (228.8 nm) of nanoparticles was obtained due to
progressive particle size reduction by sonication. Sonication
causes increase in temperature (nearly 35 degC in 5 min), which
could lead to enzyme inactivation. To prevent this, sonication
was performed in a pulsatile manner, on an ice bath. The
resting period (24 s) after sonication (8 s), provided suitable
time for dispersion of heat generated during sonication. The
uricase nanoparticles were prepared on an ice bath so that
none to minimum loss of enzymatic activity takes place. This
may be concluded on the basis of entrapment of uricase in
UNop. The entrapment efficiency of optimized batch of nanoparticles was found to be 81.26+-1.42 %, which suggests that
loss of enzyme did not take place during nanoparticles
formulation.
The nanoparticles which cross through skin are in a smaller
size range, but nanoparticles in the size range of 200-300 nm
permeate through hair follicles and other mechanisms. This
enabled localized delivery at inflamed site. The low

polydispersity index confirmed efficiency of the formulation
over particle size control. The amount of drug (enzyme)
entrapped inside the formulation determines the quality of
formulation and its utilization for dosing. The entrapment efficiency of nanoparticles depends on various parameters like

case of UNop and ANop, the drug (uricase or aceclofenac) to
polymer ratio was very high which resulted into encapsulation
of maximum drug into polymer matrix. In case of UNop, the
organic phase could also have prevented the leakage of enzyme into the aqueous dispersion medium due to low solubility of enzyme in non-aqueous solvents, during formulation.
The large volume of dispersion medium may have prevented
agglomeration of nanoparticles thereby preventing the loss of
drug (uricase or aceclofenac) which could have been
entrapped and lost through larger microparticles, if formed.
Polymer degradation can also be affected by the particle size of formulation. For instance, the degradation
rate of PLGA was found to increase with increasing
particle size. It has been reported that in smaller nanoparticles, the degradation products of PLGA diffuse out
while in larger ones, this accelerates further degradation
of PLGA . The particle size can be controlled by
altering uricase to PLGA ratio and sonication time,
