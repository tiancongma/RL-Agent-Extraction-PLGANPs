Purification processes of polymeric nanoparticles: How to improve their clinical translation?
To overcome this concern, length and number of dialysis cycles should be adjusted. For instance, PLGA-based nanospheres encapsulating either amphiphilic [132] or hydrophilic [195] complexes were purified by dialysis (cellulose membrane, MWCO= 25 kDa, volume ratio of 1:100). In the case of the amphiphilic molecules, 3 cycles of 10 h were performed, whereas only 2-h cycles were used in the case of the hydrophilic complexes, to limit the premature drug release.

PLGA-PEG nanoparticles containing gallium phthalocyanine: Preparation, optimization and analysis of its photodynamic efficiency on red blood cell and Hepa-1C1C7
The diffusion of solvent from organic phase to aqueous phase is the event that more influenced on the nanoparticle size. However, the subsequent addition of water to favor the solvent diffusion destabilize the equilibrium between organic drops into the aqueous phase, causing the diffusion of solvent carried with PLGA molecules to the aqueous phase favoring the reduce of the nanoparticles size [35,37]. The fast efflux of solvent from organic phase to aqueous phase can cause a collapse of new drops [35,38], generating the multiple size population.

Biocompatible nanoparticles and gadolinium complexes for MRI applications
The choice of this technique was guided by previous successful encapsulation of a copper analog of DOTA for which high metal loadings were achieved. In this procedure, the emulsion is composed of aqueous droplets containing GdCAs, which are dispersed inside oily drops, these oily drops being themselves dispersed in an external aqueous phase (Fig. 1a) [12,13]. The biocompatibility of the process was ensured by the use of pharmaceutically-accepted excipients, triacetin [14] as the organic solvent and poly(ethyleneglycol)30-co-poly(propyleneglycol)75-co-(polyethyleneglycol)30 (Poloxamer 188) [14] as the tensioactive.
