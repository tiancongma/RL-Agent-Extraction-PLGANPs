to dissolved CS in 0.1% acetic acid and stirred for 24 h in
order to bind FA to CS (Sigma-Aldrich). After the centrifugation of the resulting solution, the FA-CS precipitate was
prepared to determine the amount of FA binding. In order
to modify PLGA NPs with FA-CS, PLGA NPs and FA-CS
sediment were dissolved separately in deionized water, and
then, FA-CS solution was added dropwise to PLGA NPs.
After 2 h of incubation, the final mixture was centrifuged
and then lyophilized.

Arctigenin (AR) is a natural compound derived from
Arctium lappa, which is used as a medicinal supplement against many infectious diseases such as influenza.3

The glycoside compound arctiin (ARC) is known as the
main and active compound of the dried ripe fruit of A.
lappa. It has been found that these secondary metabolites
are effective in treating many diseases, including cancer,
with anti-cancer, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.4-6

However, the lack of solubility of AR in water is one
of the limitations of using this compound as an antitumor drug in new therapeutic approaches.3 Therefore, the
design of nanoparticles (NPs) containing ARC can improve
its pharmacogenetic properties.

In line with this, polylactic acid glycolic acid (PLGA)
copolymer is one of the most common compounds used
in the design of NPs to improve the drug delivery system by increasing the stability of various drugs, reducing
side effects, and optimizing the release of drugs, which
has also been approved by the FDA.7,8 However, thanks to
modifying PLGA-based NPs with other compounds such
as chitosan (CS), researchers have overcome the limitations of PLGA, such as rapid processing and lack of direct
interaction with cells or proteins.2,9 Furthermore, target

ARC-PCF-NP characterization

The results of the physicochemical characteristics of ARCPCF-NPs implied that the particle size was 100 nm, the
average hydrodynamic diameter was 291.45, the PDI index
was 0.36 nm, and the zeta potential (ZP) was 26.30 mV.
By examining the morphology of NPs by FESEM, we
found that the ARC-PCF-NPs are dispersed with spherical shapes, and the size of the particles is in accordance
with the data of DLS. The FTIR spectrum indicated a peak
at 2930 cm-1 representing N-H stretching vibration and
a peak at 1730 cm-1 for the C=O stretching vibration of
carbonyl group. In addition, the peaks in the range of 10401046 cm-1 indicated the stretching vibrations caused by
N-O and C-O bonds, respectively (Figure 1).16,17

3.5
Catalase and superoxide dismutase
gene expression

To investigate the antioxidant influence of ARC-PCFNPs on HT-29 cell lines, we investigated the expression
of superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase (CAT) after
48 h of treating the cells with ARC-PCF-NPs. Antioxidant
enzymes SOD and CAT are able to neutralize superoxide and convert H2O and O2. Changes in the antioxidant
capacity of cancer cells, including changes in SOD and
CAT gene levels, have been reported in the progression of
different cancer cells.18,19 The analyzed data implied that
increasing the concentration of ARC-PCF-NPs leads to a
decrease in the gene expression of mentioned enzymes in
colon cancer cells (Figure 5).
