Development of minoxidil-loaded double emulsion PLGA nanoparticles for the treatment of hair loss
Highlights
• Minoxidil-loaded double emulsion nanoparticles were developed for the treatment of hair loss.
• The nanoparticles showed high encapsulation efficiency and prevented aggregation at high concentrations.
• The nanoparticles promoted successful hair regrowth in mice without using ethanol and propylene glycol.

In response to the demand for increasing the stability and residence time, decreasing the toxicity and application frequency of minoxidil, nanoparticles have emerged as a new generation of topical delivery system for minoxidil, which aid in hair follicle deposition and dermal retention, minimized dermatological adverse effects and improved efficacy of therapeutic agents(Ana et al., 2020; PereiraSilva et al., 2022). Polymeric nanoparticles(Camila et al., 2020; Han et al., 2022), lipid nanoparticles(Andreas and Christel, 2015; Oliveira et al., 2022), vesicular nanoparticles(El-Zaafarany et al., 2021; Sun et al., 2023), metallic nanoparticles(Nagai et al., 2019), and cyclodextrin inclusion complexes(Angela et al., 2018; Domenico et al., 2018) are being developed and investigated for AGA treatment. In recent years, microneedle-mediated transdermal drug delivery for the management of AGA has become an emerging research focus, microneedle exhibits unique advantages such as enhanced transdermal penetration and sustained release, painless application and minimal invasiveness.

The drug-loaded polymeric NPs are synthesized using various techniques such as emulsification [14], coacervation [16], supercritical fluid [17], sol–gel [18], ionic gelation [19], and also nanoprecipitation including bulk mixing (BM) and microfluidic (MF) approaches [9,20,21].
