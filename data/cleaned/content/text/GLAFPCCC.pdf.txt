ABSTRACT
Dysregulation of pro-¬≠inflammatory cytokines participates in the initiation and development of knee osteoarthritis (OA).
Consequently, interventions to boost the anti-¬≠inflammatory capacity of articular chondrocytes have been proposed to treat early-¬≠
stage OA and prevent OA progression. Applying nanoencapsulation can enhance bioavailability and bioactivity and sustain
the anti-¬≠inflammatory activity of phytochemicals. Accordingly, in this study, we used nanocapsules to deliver curcumin (Cur)
to treat inflammatory chondrocytes. Using double-¬≠emulsion technology, Cur was encapsulated in chitosan-¬≠coated poly(lactic-¬≠
co-¬≠glycolic acid) nanocapsules. The nanocapsulized Cur (NCcur) was characterized, and the toxicity to human articular chon-
drocytes was evaluated. NCcur was applied to interleukin-¬≠1 beta (IL-¬≠1ő≤)-¬≠stimulated cells based on findings of the Cur toxicity
study. Results showed that the particle size of NCcur was 247.8‚ÄČ¬Ī‚ÄČ1.73‚ÄČnm with a zeta potential of 20.3‚ÄČ¬Ī‚ÄČ0.11‚ÄČmV and a mid-¬≠range
distribution. NCcur showed a core-¬≠shell and sphere-¬≠like morphology. The encapsulation efficiency of Cur in nanocapsules was
67.1%. Nanoencapsulation decreased the toxicity of high-¬≠dose Cur (>‚ÄČ20‚ÄČőľM), and NCcur exhibited a sustained Cur release over
72‚ÄČh. NCcur supplementation (20‚ÄČőľM) improved cell survival and ameliorated cell senescence of inflammatory chondrocytes.
The IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠induced IL1B, IL6, metalloproteinase-¬≠9 (MMP9), and MMP13 mRNA expressions were down-¬≠regulated, while IL10
level was enhanced in NCcur-¬≠treated chondrocytes. Likewise, NCcur supplementation restored aggrecan, collagen type II alpha
1 chain, and SOX9 mRNA expressions. MMP-¬≠13, IL-¬≠8, and MCP-¬≠1 secretions in the supernatant also decreased. By applying na-
nocapsules, we assumed the anti-¬≠inflammatory capacity of Cur could be sustained for treating knee OA.
1‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ|‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮIntroduction
Knee articular cartilage is known to have a limited regenera-
tive capacity, although endogenous progenitor cells were iden-
tified in the superficial zone [1]. Therefore, injury to cartilage
can cause progressive and degenerative joint diseases such as
osteoarthritis (OA) [2]. Dysregulation of pro-¬≠inflammatory cy-
tokines participates in the initiation and development of knee
OA. Catabolic mediators can amplify inflammatory cascades,
cause transformation to a hypertrophic phenotype, induce cell

senescence, and eventually lead to chondrocyte apoptosis [3].
Therefore, interventions for anti-¬≠inflammatory activities are
suggested to treat early-¬≠stage OA and prevent OA progression.
Curcumin (Cur), a turmeric-¬≠derived polyphenol, is a versatile
phytochemical isolated from Curcuma species. The chemical
and structural characteristics of Cur are crucial for its biolog-
ical activities [4]. It is a diferuloylmethane molecule, known
structurally as 1,7-¬≠bis(4-¬≠hydroxy-¬≠3-¬≠methoxyphenyl)-¬≠1,6-¬≠hept
adiene-¬≠3,5-¬≠dione. Cur exhibits a range of bioactive functions,
including anti-¬≠inflammatory, antioxidant, anticancer, and im-
munomodulatory properties¬†[5]. Cur especially showed promise
in OA treatment by suppressing inflammatory mediators of OA
chondrocytes in¬†vitro [6]. Moreover, Cur was shown to slow OA
progression, relieve pain scores, and modulate extracellular ma-
trix (ECM) homeostasis in various animal models [7, 8]. In ad-
dition, oral administration of Cur is a recommended adjunctive
treatment for knee OA due to pain relief effects [9]. Despite Cur
showing solid evidence of chondroprotective ability, it is also
known that the bioavailability of orally administered Cur is low,
and the absorption of Cur results in rapid loss in the gastrointes-
tinal tract [10].
Applying nanotechnologies can improve the bioavailability
and bioactivity and sustain the antioxidant activities of phyto-
chemicals [11]. Advances in nanoparticle fabrication have also
benefited Cur-¬≠based complexes as effective therapeutic agents
[12]. Nanoparticles can be classified into nanospheres and nano-
capsules based on their structural organization. A nanocapsule
is a core-¬≠shell structure with active agents entrapped within
the core. The polymeric wall commonly consists of biodegrad-
able materials that are important in controlling drug release.
Advantages of using nanocapsules as a drug delivery system
include a low burst effect, low drug irritation, and a high load-
ing capacity [13, 14]. Poly(lactic-¬≠co-¬≠glycolic acid) (PLGA), a bio-
degradable copolymer, has been approved by the US Food and
Drug Administration for drug delivery systems. In the human
body, hydrolysis of PLGA produces lactic acid and glycolic acid,
which can easily be metabolized through the citric acid cycle
[15, 16]. In addition to drug delivery, PLGA has been widely used
as a cell carrier for cartilage repair [17]. Chitosan is a biodegrad-
able polysaccharide consisting of ő≤-¬≠(1-¬≠4)-¬≠linked d-¬≠glucosamine
and N-¬≠acetyl-¬≠d-¬≠glucosamine. The structure of chitosan is sim-
ilar to native polysaccharides comprising articular cartilage
[18]. Several studies showed that chitosan can promote cartilage
tissue formation by increasing glycosaminoglycan (GAG) and
Type II collagen secretion by chondrocytes [19]. Accordingly,
chitosan-¬≠coated PLGA nanocapsules were fabricated as a drug
delivery carrier of Cur. Therapeutic potentials of nanocaps-
ulized Cur (NCcur) toward human OA chondrocytes were eval-
uated in this study.

Chitosan-¬≠coated PLGA nanocapsules containing Cur were

4‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ|‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮDiscussion
Applying phytochemicals was proposed as a way to modulate in-
flammatory cascades in the pathological progression of OA [22].
Despite recognized chondroprotective effects, Cur is known to
be rapidly degraded in physiological conditions and has a low
bioavailability. On the contrary, Cur packed in nanocapsules
showed superior anti-¬≠edematogenic activity without impairing
normal hepatic functions in an animal model of arthritis [23]. In
addition, Cur-¬≠loaded nanocapsules were also found to exert an
antioxidant capacity and promote M2 macrophage polarization
[24]. Therefore, delivering NCcur could be a promising approach
for treating knee OA.

It is known that different encapsulation strategies can influ-
ence the encapsulation efficiency of bioactive molecules. In
addition, high doses of Cur can be cytotoxic to cells. On the
contrary, utilizing a nanocarrier can prevent burst release and
achieve sustained release of curcumin, thereby enhancing its
biocompatibility and bioavailability [25]. In the double emul-
sion method, hydrophobic molecules are incorporated into the
oil phase together with PLGA, which minimizes drug leak-
age during the emulsification process [26]. This approach fa-
cilitates the effective entrapment of hydrophobic compounds
within nanocapsules, resulting in improved encapsulation
efficiency.
The double emulsion technique produced nanocapsules with a
spherical shape and a core-¬≠shell structure (Figure¬† 1a), which
could facilitate cell uptake and enable high amounts of hydro-
phobic drug encapsulation in the oil core [27]. Encapsulation
of Cur in nanocapsules slightly increased the particle size
(Figure¬† 1b). Since Cur is a neutral compound, nanoencapsu-
lation did not change its zeta potential. A relatively small PDI
and high zeta potential can reduce spontaneous nanoparticle
aggregation [28]. Nanoparticles with a cationic surface charge
can bond with the negatively charged cartilage ECM through
electrostatic attraction. Moreover, cationic nanoparticles usu-
ally exhibit higher cell uptake than neutral or anionic nanopar-
ticles due to attractive electrostatic interactions with negatively
charged cell membranes [29]. After an initial burst release in the
first 3‚ÄČh, Cur was released from the NC in a stable concentration,
as shown by the in¬†vitro drug release pattern (Figure¬†1c). The
early release of hydrophobic Cur was mainly controlled by the
diffusion mechanism. Continuous release of Cur was observed
over a period of 72‚ÄČh, revealing that its bioavailability could be
improved. The time to reach 100% release might be accompa-
nied by degradation of the polymer matrix [30]. Taken together,
positively charged NCcur with a core-¬≠shell structure may have
low burst release, a long residence time, and high cellular uptake
that could maintain the drug level within the therapeutic range
for a relatively long period.

The IL-¬≠1ő≤ pro-¬≠inflammatory cytokine is known as a critical
factor that contributes to OA progression, and thus, IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠
stimulated articular chondrocytes are a well-¬≠accepted in¬†vitro
OA cell model [34]. Both Cur and NCcur supplementations
ameliorated IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠induced cell death (Figure¬† 3b), while the
mitochondrial activity of chondrocytes did not fully recover
(Figure¬†3a). Chen et¬†al. pretreated rat chondrocytes with Cur
and then exposed cells to IL-¬≠1ő≤ [35]. They reported that Cur
pre-¬≠treatment ameliorated IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠induced apoptosis, while cell
death (LDH release level) was still higher than in normal cells.
In addition, IL-¬≠1ő≤ is also known to cause cell apoptosis and se-
nescence [36]. Our results revealed that NCcur supplementation
relieved cell death (Figure¬†4b) and cell senescence (Figure¬†4d)
of IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠stimulated chondrocytes. Similarly, Deng et¬†al. noted
that 10‚ÄČőľM Cur could alleviate senescence of bone marrow mes-

which NCcur affects cell survival and senescence should be fur-
ther validated.
NCcur supplementation decreased IL-¬≠lő≤-¬≠induced IL1B, IL6,
MMP9, and MMP13 mRNA levels and restored reductions in
ACAN and COL2A1 expressions (Figure¬† 5), which was simi-
lar to the biological actions of Cur on chondrocytes [10]. These
findings reveal that the nanocapsules did not influence the bio-
active functions of Cur, and NCcur could regulate inflamma-
tory reactions and modulate ECM-¬≠related genes in particular
chondrocytes. Moreover, NCcur enhanced mRNA expression
of the IL10 anti-¬≠inflammatory cytokine by IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠treated chon-
drocytes. D'Angeli et¬†al. also reported that poly(őĶ-¬≠caprolactone)
NCcur down-¬≠regulated IL1B and up-¬≠regulated IL10 mRNA lev-
els in lipopolysaccharide-¬≠treated adipose-¬≠derived stem cells [38].
Furthermore, the chondrogenic marker SOX9, vital in maintain-
ing the functional phenotype of chondrocytes, was restored in
NCcur-¬≠treated inflammatory chondrocytes. Wang et¬†al. showed
similar findings of Cur up-¬≠regulating SOX9 mRNA expression
by IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠stimulated mouse knee chondrocytes [39]. Therefore,
NCcur can modulate inflammatory mediators and enhance
chondrogenic and ECM-¬≠related gene expressions, which are im-
portant to cartilage homeostasis.
A limitation of this study is the inconsistent findings of pro-¬≠
inflammatory cytokine detection. Deng et¬† al. reported that
Cur decreased IL-¬≠1ő≤ and IL-¬≠6 levels in human articular chon-
drocytes [40]. Likewise, Cur-¬≠loaded micelles decreased IL-¬≠1ő≤
and TNF-¬≠őĪ levels in inflamed joints of mice [41]. However, our
results of ELISA kits and inflammation antibody array showed
that IL-¬≠1ő≤ and IL-¬≠6 secretion levels were not decreased in cul-
ture supernatants despite IL1B and IL6 mRNA expressions
being down-¬≠regulated in NCcur-¬≠supplied inflammatory chon-
drocytes (Figure¬†6). The IL-¬≠6 signaling pathway is recognized
as a crucial factor in the inflammatory response associated
with OA [42]. Otherwise, NCcur decreased MMP-¬≠13 in IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠
stimulated human chondrocytes in this study, similar to the
functions of Cur that Wang et¬†al. found in rat chondrocytes [43].
Moreover, we found that NCcur decreased the secretions of IL-¬≠8
and MCP-¬≠1 while restoring TIMP-¬≠2 and enhancing PDGF-¬≠BB
production, indicating that NCcur may benefit cartilage ho-
meostasis [44]. However, another critical chemokine involved
in cartilage degradation, RANTES, was increased upon NCcur
supplementation [45]. Therefore, the modulation of NCcur on
cytokine and chemokine levels should be further verified.
While NCcur shows chondroprotective effects on IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠
stimulated human articular chondrocytes, nanoencapsulation
may change the biological activity of Cur. When compared to
the free form, the bioaccessibility and bioactivity of Cur de-
creased after encapsulation [46]. Therefore, the therapeutic
potentials of Cur and NCcur shall be compared. Additionally,
intra-¬≠articular administration is a proposed route for delivering
NCcur. A knee OA animal model should be used to assess the
retention of NCcur in the damaged cartilage and its in¬†vivo ther-
apeutic effects in the future.
5‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ|‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮ‚ÄĮConclusions
Chitosan-¬≠coated PLGA NCcur was fabricated using a double-¬≠
emulsion method with a core-¬≠shell and sphere-¬≠like structure,
a particle size of 247.8‚ÄČ¬Ī‚ÄČ1.73‚ÄČnm, and a positive zeta potential
of 20.3‚ÄČ¬Ī‚ÄČ0.11‚ÄČmV. Nanoencapsulation decreased the high-¬≠dose
toxicity of Cur, and NCcur showed a sustained Cur release.
NCcur supplementation enhanced cell survival and alleviated
cell senescence of IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠treated chondrocytes. IL-¬≠1ő≤-¬≠induced
IL1B, IL6, MMP9, and MMP13 mRNA expressions were down-¬≠

also decreased MMP-¬≠13, IL-¬≠8, and MCP-¬≠1 secretions, while IL-¬≠
1ő≤ and IL-¬≠6 productions did not decrease. NCcur may improve
homeostasis of the knee joint and has the potential to be applied
as OA therapy.
