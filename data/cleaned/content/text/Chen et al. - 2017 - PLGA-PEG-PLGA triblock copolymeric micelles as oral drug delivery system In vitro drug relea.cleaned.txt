2.2. Synthesis of PLGA-PEG-PLGA and US597@micelles

PLGA (0.1 mmol) was dissolved in 5 mL anhydrous dichloromethane (DCM), after that, N,N0-dicyclohexylcarbodiimide (DCC)
(0.8 mmol) and N-Hydroxysuccinimide (NHS) (0.8 mmol) were
added in the solution. The mixture was then stirred at room temperature for 16 h under N2, and filtered through a 0.45 lm PTFE
syringe filter to remove the dicyclohexylurea (DCU) from product.
The precipitate was then added to cold anhydrous ether and stirred
at 0 C for 30 min. The solution was filtered through a PTFE syringe
filter and freeze-dried under vacuum overnight to obtain the solidified activated PLGA.
The triblock polymer PLGA-PEG-PLGA micelles were prepared
from activated PLGA, DCM, and PEG-bis-amine. Briefly, Activated

micelles , gold nanoparticles  and quantum dots  have
been investigated as potential oral drug delivery systems. Copolymers containing polyethylene glycol (PEG) and PLGA blocks
(now approved by the FDA for human use) are attracting great
interest both in academia and industry for biomedical applications
due to their biodegradable nature and biocompatible properties
. Moreover, PEG and PLGA have been employed as a drug
delivery system to improve the pharmacokinetic profiles of small
molecules. Gref et al.  concluded PEG-coated particles as a controlled intravenous delivery for hydrophobic drugs, which could
increase blood circulation times and reduced liver accumulation.
