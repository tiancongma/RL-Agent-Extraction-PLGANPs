2.3.3. Differential scanning Calorimetry(DSC)
Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) was used to determine the
glass transition temperature (Tg) of the microspheres. Modulated DSC
was performed with the cycle below: Approximately 10 mg of each
sample in an aluminum pan was heated from 20 to 280 *C at a rate of
10 *C/min under 20 mL/min nitrogen purge. The DSC thermograms
were analyzed using the associated TA Instrument's Universal Analysis
software package. DSC thermograms were obtained from blank micro
spheres, LNG drugs, LNG-PLGA microsphere and the polymer/LNG
physical mixture. The physical mixture was prepared by mixing PLGA
and drug and grinding to achieve a homogeneous mixture.

2.2.3. Preparation of LNG-PLGA-PCL implant
Implant was prepared using a TDP-5B single punch tablet press
(Shanghai, China) based on microspheres. Briefly, 79 mg microspheres
were placed in the die cavity of a single punch tablet press machine,
equipped with 6 mm flat faced punches. Then, the compression force of
the tablet machine was adjusted to produce tablets of hardness ranging
between 2 and 3 kg.
A certain amount of PCL was dissolved with acetone to achieving
coating solution (w/w, 2%, 3%, 4%, 5%, 6%, respectively). The tablet
was fixed to the device and sprayed with the coating solution, dried for
30 min at room temperature. Subsequently, another side of tablet was
performed with the same procedure. To ensure weight consistency of
each tablet, the coated tablet was weighed. The weight of coating ma
terials covered tablet was not allowed to exceed 2.4 mg (3% weight of
tablet). The implantable tablet with better coating formation was
selected for further experiment. The better implantable tablet should
have a smooth surface and the weight difference of the tablet before and
after coating should not exceed 3%. All the instruments in procedures
were sterilized with 75% ethanol solution.

2.4. In vitro drug release

In vitro release of LNG-loaded microspheres was performed on dial

bag, and added 3 mL PBS solution. fasten dialysis bag containing LNG
microspheres were put into the centrifuge tube with 40 mL PBS solution
(pH 7.4, containing 0.1% Tween 80 and 0.01% benzalkonium bromide).
The implant was placed in the centrifuge tube with same release medium
directly. The centrifuge tube was placed on the temperature shaker
(ZWYR-2102C Constant temperature culture oscillator, Shanghai Zhi
cheng Analytical Instrument Manufacturing Co., Ltd.) at 100 rpm and
37 *C. Samples were taken at 0.5 h, 1 h, 2 h, 4 h, 6 h, 8 h, 12 h, 1d, 2d, 4d,
8d, 15d, 22d, 29d, 36d, 42d, 49d, 56d, 63d, respectively. 1 mL of release
medium was taken each time, and PBS solution of the same volume was
added to maintain the sink condition. The obtained sample solution was
centrifuged at 6, 000 rpm for 10 min, and the supernatant was taken and
filtered through 0.45 mm filter membrane, the continuous filtrate was
analyzed by HPLC. The drug content was determined and the cumula
tive release of the drug in vitro was calculated.
Zero model, first model, Higuchi model and Ritger-peppas release
model was used to fit the in vitro release data of implantable tablet, and
the correlation coefficient R2 was used to judge the fitting degree .
The closer R2 is to 1, the higher the fitting degree is.

3.4. In vivo pharmacokinetics study

The LNG-PLGA-PCL implant coated with 5% PCL solution were
selected for the subsequent animal experiment. As shown in Fig. 8, the
concentration-time (C-T) curve of plasma drug of LNG-PLGA micro
spheres showed a bimodal phenomenon. The LNG concentration in
blood reached maximum concentration (Cmax) on the first day after
administration and some surge was observed in following three weeks.
After that, the LNG concentration in plasma decreased rapidly in the
following 40d. This could be because of the degradation of LNG-PLGA
microspheres, there is a fluctuation of the blood LNG concentration in
the first three weeks, after which the blood LNG concentration decreases
sharply. The release amount of LNG-PLGA-PCL implant at first day was
lower than that of LNG-PLGA microspheres, and LNG release behavior
during the whole release period was similar to that of commercial LNG-
silicone rods. Within 2 months, the concentrations of the two subcu
taneous implants in rat plasma were relatively stable, without obvious
peak and valley fluctuations. It indicates that LNG-PLGA-PCL implant
displayed a long-term stable release in vivo, which conforms to its in vitro
release profile.
