It is a safe, prominent therapeutic molecule, which is widely

a solution or in powder form for delivery through nasal and

hampers its absorption across the gastrointestinal tract (GIT),

In order to circumvent these thorny limitations of existing
delivery systems, an advanced, polymeric-lipid hybrid nano-
particles (PLHNs) based tunable, robust architecture has been
impressively proposed, in which a lipid layer envelopes the
polymeric core. PLHNs are colloidal hybrid carrier systems,
which comprise of positive attributes of both, liposomes as well
as polymeric nanoparticles in a single vehicle.18 The polymeric
core confers the structural integrity to the PLHNs whereas the
lipid envelop imparts the biomimetic shield. The enhanced
encapsulation yield for hydrophilic drug could be anticipated by
lipidic monolayer coating over the surface of the polymeric core
during the preparation. It has also been reported that molecular
barrier conferred by lipid, impedes the faster diÔ¨Äusion of
hydrophilic drug into an external aqueous phase and hence,
potentially increases drug encapsulation yield.19 Literature
survey reveals that number of PLHNs has been prepared to
enhance the encapsulation of hydrophilic drug, compared to
plain polymeric nanoparticles without any lipidic surfactant.14,19
Moreover, PLHNs have also shown prolongation of the drug
release owing to their core‚Äďshell type of structure, which
restricts inward penetration rate of water.20 Previous studies
have described the use of PLHNs for enhancing the oral
permeability as well as bioavailability.14,21,22 The enhanced oral
permeation of PLHNs is attributed to their absorption through
paracellular and transcellular route via enterocytes as well as
selective uptake by M-cells of Peyer's Patches (PP).21,22 To our
knowledge, there have been no reports available in literature
pertaining oral bioavailability enhancement of CS, by preparing
nanocarrier system till date.
In this perspective, present study was aimed at the utiliza-
tion of the potential of PLHNs for oral delivery of CS, which
would encapsulate the hydrophilic CS with high payload and
