absence of the optimal dosage studies of TCP-PLGA microspheres in that kind of experimental models.
It is largely assumed that the eYcacy of a drug in animal

resultant eVects of antibiotics. However, relevance to

## Conclusion
The in vitro and in vivo studies had shown
that the TCP-PLGA microspheres were eVective for the
treatment of chronic osteomyelitis in an animal experimental model. Hence, these microspheres may be potentially
useful in the clinical setting with the need for further investigation for optimal dosing of TCP-PLGA microspheres.

Keywords
TCP-PLGA implants * Osteomyelitis * MRSA

This study was conducted to investigate the use of PLGA
microspheres to deliver a clinically eVective concentration
of TCP for a prolonged period in order to eradicate MRSA
infections in chronic osteomyelitis. Previous studies had
shown that most of the antibiotic is permanently trapped in
the bone cement, resulting in poor drug release. The major
disadvantage of such non-biodegradable systems is the
need for a secondary surgery to remove the implant upon
completion of the antibiotic release. In contrast, these biodegradable carriers do not require post-treatment surgical
intervention to remove the spent implant. Importantly,
these biodegradable TCP-PLGA microspheres (TMS4)
implanted to proximal tibia of the rats are clinically eVective, thereby strongly supporting the exploitation of these
biodegradable delivery systems for the local treatment of
chronic osteomyelitis. Further research into the determination of optimal dosage of applied antibiotics for the eVective total eradication of MRSA and the possibility for
development of antimicrobial resistance upon prolonged
use of these delivery systems are ongoing.
