Abstract
Nitrogen-containing heterocyclic compounds are currently used for a number of pharmaceutical and
agricultural applications because they have biological activities such as antimicrobial, antiviral,
antituberculosis, anticancer, analgesic, antioxidant, anti-inﬂammatory and antidepressant. 1,2,3,4-
Tetrazines and 1,2,3-triazoles are examples of high-nitrogen heterocyclic compounds. Coumarins, on
the other hand, are lactones that form a group of oxygenated heterocyclic compounds found in plants.
In this article, two analogs of 1,2,3,4-tetrazine, two analogs of 1,2,3-triazole and ﬁve analogs of
coumarin were designed and synthesized. Their chemical structures were characterized by detecting
their FTIR, 1H-NMR, and 13C-NMR (APT) spectra. The antioxidant activities of all synthesized
molecules were compared at a ﬁxed concentration (0.25 mg ml−1) using the 1,1-diphenyl-2-
picrylhydrazyl (DPPH) method. Molecules 9c and 9e, which showed the highest antioxidant activity,
were loaded into PLGA (poly(lactic-co-glycolic) acid) nanoparticles using the oil in water (o/w) single
emulsion solvent evaporation method as a model study. Synthesized nanoparticles characterized for
particle size, zeta potential, functional groups, morphology, and release properties. Particle size and
zeta potential of 9c/NP were determined as 216.1 ± 8.944 nm and −14.1 ± 2.40 mV, respectively. The
particle size and zeta potential for 9e/NP were measured as 222.0 ± 12.490 nm and −12.4 ± 1.42 mV
respectively. The study results obtained on model nanoparticle systems with elucidated physicochem-
ical properties may have the potential to provide a promising basis for oxidative stress-related diseases
in the future.
1. Introduction
Tetrazine is a compound that consists of a six-membered aromatic ring containing four nitrogen atoms, with the
molecular formula C2H2N4. Tetrazine has three isomers: 1,2,4,5-tetrazine, 1,2,3,4-tetrazine, and 1,2,3,5-
tetrazine [1]. These compounds, with adjacent multiple nitrogen atoms, are important organic molecules found
in many biologically active substances [2]. Therefore, the development of various effective methods to obtain
heterocycles with tetrazine structure has attracted great attention in recent years. Azides serve as valuable
nitrogen sources in the synthesis of 1,2,3,4-tetrazine molecules [3, 4]. Tetrazines with adjacent N,N,N,N
structures have been reported to have many biological effects such as anticancer [5, 6]. Therefore, studies to
synthesize 1,2,3,4-tetrazine and its derivatives from azides and to develop new methods have recently gained
momentum [7].

of the cycloaddition reaction of the azaoxyallyl cation intermediate produced by base-mediated

Among nitrogen-containing heterocyclic compounds, triazole derivatives also have a wide spectrum of
activity. 1,2,3-triazole and 1,2,4-triazole derivatives, which have two different triazole structures, play an
important role in the synthesis of new bioactive compounds as they contain various electrophiles and
nucleophiles. Triazole and its derivatives have important biological activities such as antimicrobial, antiviral,
antituberculosis, anticancer, analgesic, antioxidant, anti-inﬂammatory and antidepressant [17]. Triazoles can be
produced by addition of [3+2] cycloaddition with azide and alkyne in a transition metal-catalyzed click reaction
such as Ru, Cu, Rh, Ag, Ir [18–22]. And also, azides give [3+2] cycloaddition reactions with nitriles, amides
[23–27].
Coumarin is composed of a benzopyran-δ-lactone skeleton [28] and exhibits a large number of bioactivities
[29, 30]. When the coumarin acts as a substituent, it can also increase the bioactivity of the whole molecule
[31, 32]. Most coumarin derivatives act as various agents and inhibitors. In addition to their low toxicity and side
effects, they are also known to have anticancer activity. For this purpose, various highly effective and
pharmacologically important coumarin drug candidates are being evaluated at the clinical stage [33].
Coumarin was ﬁrst isolated from tonka beans by Vogel in 1820 [34]. By its structure, it consists of an α-
pyrone ring fused with a benzene ring. Coumarin is considered the simplest form of a large class of naturally
occurring phenolic substances [35]. Coumarin compounds have anti-inﬂammatory [36, 37], antibacterial
[38–40], antiviral [41], antioxidant [42–44], anti-Alzheimer’s Disease (AD) [45, 46], antitumor effects [47–50] It
has pharmaceutical and biological properties such as inhibition of cyclooxygenases [51, 52], antithrombotic
[53], inhibition of lipoxygenase [52, 54] and inhibition of xanthine oxidase [55].
Drug delivery systems are employed to effectively transport drugs within the body, ensuring the desired
therapeutic outcome while maintaining safety. Typically, these systems are engineered to enhance the solubility
of active compounds in water, bolster their chemical stability, increase pharmacological activity, and mitigate
any potential side effects. Among various drug carrier systems, nanoparticles have emerged as promising
candidates in recent years [56]. Polymeric materials have garnered signiﬁcant attention in the realm of
biomedical applications as carriers for drugs. Extensive research has been conducted on these materials, leading
to a wide range of options. Suitable polymeric drug delivery platforms can be selected based on speciﬁc drug
types and routes of administration. Polymeric nanoparticles, in particular, have been extensively studied as
carrier systems for various bioactive drugs, including hydrophobic and hydrophilic drugs, nucleic acids,
peptides, and proteins. The distinct structure and composition of polymeric NPs offer numerous advantages
over free drug formulations [57]. These advantages include extending the plasma half-life of the drug, keeping
the plasma concentration of the drug constant within the therapeutic window for the desired period of time,
eliminating the possible toxic effects of the drug by obtaining higher biological activity at lower drug doses,
protecting drugs against enzymatic degradation, and increasing the solubility of hydrophobic drugs [58, 59].
PLGA is extensively utilized in the development and composition of drug delivery systems due to its
biocompatibility and biodegradability [60, 61]. Moreover, the degradation byproducts of PLGA are non-toxic,
as PLGA is broken down into its constituent lactic acid and glycolic acid monomers, which participate in the
citric acid cycle in the physiological environment and are metabolized to H2O and CO2 and also remains inert in
the physiological environment [62]. In addition, it has been approved by regulatory authorities such as the
American Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and the European Medicines Agency (EMA) for use in various
applications such as inﬂammation, cancer, and diabetes [63]. Due to these advantages, PLGA was preferred as
the model polymer in the preparation of carrier nanoparticles for use within the scope of the presented study.
In this study, it was aimed to ﬁrst synthesize a series of new 1,2,3,4-tetrazine, 1,2,3-triazole and coumarin
derivatives that may have antioxidant activity and to elucidate their structures using spectroscopic methods. It
was then aimed to perform antioxidant activity tests of these new compounds and to apply nanoparticle
encapsulation as a model system to improve the properties of highly active compounds. In this context, a series
of novel heterocyclic compound derivatives were synthesized, their antioxidant activity properties were
determined, and nanoparticle encapsulation was applied.

2.1. Chemical synthesis
Information about the reactions carried out under inert atmosphere was stated in the general synthesis method
and reaction conditions. Reagents and solvents purchased from Sigma Aldrich or Merck were used without any
further processing. Dry acetone was distilled by boiling with CaH under nitrogen atmosphere and kept in 4 A
molecular sieve. Dry N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF) was prepared by drying on a 4 A molecular sieve

2.2. Synthesis of bicyclo[2.2.1]hept-8-ene-3-endo,5-endodicarboximide (1) [64]
Maleimide (7.28 g, 75.0 mmol, 1.0 equiv) was weighed into a single-necked ﬂask and dissolved in diethyl

2.3. Synthesis of N-(benzyloxy)-2-bromo-2-methylpropanamide (2) [8]
O-Benzylhydroxylamine hydrochloride (1.56 g, 10 mmol) and triethylamine (1.01 g, 10 mmol) was added to a
solution of 2-bromo-2-methylpropanoyl bromide (2.3 g, 10 mmol) dissolved in dichloromethane (0.5 M) at
0 °C. After the reaction mixture was stirred at 0 °C overnight, it was quenched with water and extraction was
performed. The organic phase was dried over Na2SO4, ﬁltered, and evaporated under reduced pressure.
Puriﬁcation was performed with the help of column chromatography (3:1, n-hexane:EtOAc). A white solid was
obtained in 87% yield. Rf = 0.58 (3:1, Hexanes: Ethyl Acetate); mp = 88 °C–92 °C; FTIR (ATR): ν = 3192, 3036,
2956, 2890, 1651, 1497, 1469, 1454, 1112, 1031, 1004 cm−1. 1H-NMR (500 MHz, CDCl3): δ 9.02 (brs, 1H),
7.37–7.31 (m, 5H), 4.87 (s, 2H), and 1.86 (s, 6H).
2.4. General procedure for 3a-b
The dihaloalkane (1,4-dibromobutane, 1,6-dibromohexane or 1,8-dibromooctane) (4 mmol) was added to a
solution of compound 1 (1 mmol) and potassium carbonate in dry 2-butanone (20 ml) or dry
dimethylformamide (10 ml) under inert atmosphere, and the reaction mixture was reﬂuxed at 155 °C for
4–24 h. The reaction was monitored with the TLC system. The mixture was brought to room temperature and
ﬁltered. The organic solvent was removed under reduced pressure and the crude product was puriﬁed by
column chromatography to obtain compounds 3a-b, respectively.

2.5. General procedure for 4a-b

vacuum. The substance was used directly in the next reaction.

2.11.1. Antioxidant activity evaluation of compounds
DPPH, being a stable free radical, has the ability to accept an electron or hydrogen, resulting in its conversion
into a molecule that is both neutral and stable. In DPPH method, the ability of free compounds to donate
hydrogen or electron atoms was evaluated visually according to the amount of color change of the purple
DPPH solution to the yellow solution and spectrophotometrically by measuring the absorbance at 517 nm
[72–74]. First, an ethanolic solution of DPPH (0.1 mM; 0.5 mg of DPPH in 13 ml of ethanol) was prepared and
its absorbance at 517 nm (to be used as a control) was immediately measured using a UV–Vis
spectrophotometer (UV-1700 Pharmaspec, Shimadzu, Japan). Then, compounds with a concentration of
0.25 mg ml−1 were added to the DPPH solution at a ratio of 1:5 (sample:DPPH; v/v) and vortexed. The reaction
solution was incubated in the dark at room temperature for 30 min. After this period, spectrophotometric
measurements of the DPPH solutions treated with the samples were carried out at 517 nm. The antioxidant
activity was expressed as the percentage of scavenging effect and the DPPH scavenging effect were calculated by
substituting in the equation (1).

2.11.2. Synthesis of nanoparticles
Because they show higher antioxidant activity than others, 9c and 9e loaded nanoparticles were synthesized. The
process of synthesizing 9c or 9e-loaded PLGA NPs was carried out by employing the oil in water (o/w) single
emulsion solvent evaporation technique, as described in the available literature [75]. Brieﬂy, 6 mg of 9c or 9e was
dissolved in 1 ml of DCM (Ridel de Haen) and 20 mg of PLGA was dissolved in 1 ml of DCM. Subsequently, the
two solutions were merged and thoroughly mixed. The resulting organic phase was slowly added to the external
aqueous phase consisting of 4 ml of 3% (w/v) PVA solution. This was performed using a microtip probe
sonicator (Bandelin Sonopuls, Germany) placed in an ice bath, resulting in the formation of a single emulsion.
Then, the oil/water emulsion was mixed with 35 ml of 0.1% (w/v) PVA solution and stirred for 3 h at room
temperature to allow the organic solvent in the organic phase to evaporate. The particles formed were
subsequently gathered through centrifugation (Nüve 800 R) at a speed of 9000 rpm, then washed three times
with ultrapure water to efﬁciently eliminate any PVA and unencapsulated compounds. Following this, the

2.11.4. In vitro release study
The 9c or 9e release from NPs in vitro was studied using the dissolution method [81]. During the release study,
lyophilized 9c/NP or 9e/NP were resuspended in PBS (pH 7.4, 0.01 M) to mimic physiological pH, with ﬁnal
concentrations of molecules set to 0.5 mg ml−1 based on loading capacity. The release study was also repeated in
PBS buffer containing 1% (v/v) Tween 20 (used as surfactant). The suspension obtained was then subjected to
incubation in a shaker at 37 °C and 150 rpm. The suspensions were subjected to centrifugation at 9,000 rpm for
20 min at different predetermined time intervals (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 h; 1, 2, 3, 4, 7, 11 days). Following centrifugation,
the supernatant was meticulously collected, while the pellet was subsequently resuspended in PBS. This process
was repeated until the release of compounds from NPs was completed. The amount of 9c or 9e in the
supernatant was determined by measuring the absorbance at 330 nm for 9c and 321 nm for 9e using a UV–vis
spectrophotometer and then referring to a previously prepared calibration curve.

In developing the novel antioxidative agent, we focused on the tetrazine, triazole and coumarin derivatives. For
this purpose, our synthesis started with the preparing compound 1 with known procedure at room temperature
in 100% conversion [64] (Scheme 1). Maleimide reacted with freshly distillated cyclopentadiene in ethylacetate

hydrogen free radicals is an indicator of its antioxidant activity [82].

4. Conclusions
As a result, the synthesis of bicyclic heterocyclic compounds containing nitrogen atoms such as tetrazine,
triazole, and coumarin has been reported. The antioxidant activities of these compounds were compared at a
constant concentration using the DPPH method, and nanoparticle encapsulation was carried out for molecules
with high activity. The results obtained indicate that among the nine synthesized coumarin derivatives,
compounds 9c and 9e exhibit the highest antioxidant activity due to their ability to donate H radicals.
Furthermore, the resonance stability in molecule 9e facilitates easier breakdown of the allylic radical hydrogen,
enhancing its antioxidant activity by increasing its propensity to donate hydrogen radicals. Therefore, the
ﬁndings of this study suggest the development of a promising platform for therapeutic applications related to
oxidative stress and may contribute to future research in this area.
