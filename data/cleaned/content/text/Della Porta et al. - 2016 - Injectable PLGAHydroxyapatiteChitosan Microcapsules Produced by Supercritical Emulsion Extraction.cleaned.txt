osteoblast cells adhered to HA preferentially, leading to subsequent

SEE Processing of Emulsions

In the SEE process, the scope of the packed column is to provide
a very large surface for contact between the falling liquid phase and
the going up gas phase (liquid emulsion and supercritical extracting
agent, respectively); as a consequence, SC-CO2 rapidly diffuses into
the oily phase of emulsion, causing the formation of an "expanded"
oily phase droplet in where the solvent is rapidly extracted. A fast
polymer hardening is obtained with the formation of solid microspheres (see SEE process illustration in Fig. 1). Water is not miscible
with the carbon dioxide at the operative conditions selected, and
the microspheres can be continuously recovered at the bottom of
the column after only 4 min of residence time into the apparatus.

Chitosan Covering of PLGA Microdevices

In addition to particle morphology and size, the surface charge is
considered one of the most important parameters governing
microcapsule versus tissue interactions. Zeta potential is a useful
indicator of surface charge, and it can be used as an index of the

## Conclusion
SEE confirmed to be a green, flexible, and robust technology to
fabricate multiloaded microcapsules, coupling the efficiency of
continuous operation to easy process scalability. Both Gen and THA
molecules were not chemically modified or degraded by the
encapsulation technology and should maintain their biological
activity. The emulsion formulations explored, coupled with the
supercritical technology, can be used to fabricate versatile microdevices, able to control the drug release over a wide range of time
and concentrations, depending on specific applications. Moreover,
the "burst" effect was largely reduced when chitosan coating was
added. Good encapsulation efficiency and high reproducibility
mean sizes will also ensure efficient and reliable drug release
profiles, as well as, the possibility of delay or accelerate the drugs
delivery in dependence of the required target.
A perspective of this work will be the sustained delivery of THA
or THA/Gen for 15-20 days directly at the site where its biological
action is required, instead of a systemic and daily dose injection.
Alternatively, because PLGA microspheres were recently proposed
as a spacer into the apatite calcium phosphate cement to induce
macroporosity56,57 or provide acid degradation products that
seemed to accelerate calcium phosphate cement degradation,58 the
described microcapsules may be also used in orthopedical surgery
formulations as multipurpose microfillers not only able to generate
macropores but, additionally, to deliver THA or Gen/THA in situ
enhancing bone ingrowth, especially in aged patients. Future
studies will involve the in vivo testing of the proposed formulations
in a rat model of fracture healing.
