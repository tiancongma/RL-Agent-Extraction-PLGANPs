Cisplatin is widely used for the treatment of various cancers including cervical, ovarian, lung and head and neck,
however, its clinical success is limited owing to the dose-dependent adverse effects, mainly nephrotoxicity and
neurotoxicity. In order to address this limitation, the present study was undertaken to investigate growth inhibitory effect of cisplatin in combination with a triterpenediol (3a, 24-dihydroxyurs-12-ene and 3a, 24dihydroxyolean-12-ene, TPD) on human ovarian cancer cell line. Poly(dl-lactic-co-glycolic) acid nanoparticles
loaded with TPD (TPD-PLGA-NPs) were successfully developed by emulsion solvent evaporation method. The
TPD-PLGA-NPs were characterized for size distribution and zeta potential which was in order of 152.56 +-
3.01 nm and -17.36 +- 0.37 mV respectively. The morphological evaluation was carried out by transmission
electron microscopy and the formulation was also characterized using Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy
(FTIR) and differential scanning calorimetry (DSC). The drug loading of the optimized formulation was
51.03 +- 1.52 mg/mg and the formulation exhibited sustained drug release profile. The in vitro cellular uptake
study of coumarin-6 loaded PLGA nanoparticles in OVCAR-5 cells demonstrated a time dependent increase in uptake efficiency. Further, growth inhibitory effect of cisplatin was investigated in combination with TPD-PLGANPs. The combination index (CI) was b1, indicating a synergistic interaction. Further, at 75% of cell growth inhibition (ED75) the dose of cisplatin was reduced to 3.8 folds using this combination. The results indicated the potential of cisplatin and TPD-PLGA-NPs combination in order to reduce to dose limiting toxicities of the former.

2.5.3. In vitro growth inhibition assay
The sulforhodamine B (SRB) assay was performed to evaluate in
vitro growth inhibition of cisplatin, TPD and TPD-PLGA-NPs and their
combinations in the selected cell line . Briefly, cells suspension
(100 mL) were added in 96-well tissue culture plate at a density of
8000-12,000 cells per well 24 h prior to drug treatment. The cells
were exposed with different concentration of free drug, nano-formulation and their combinations with fixed molar ratio. Following 48 h treatment cells were fixed with ice-cold trichloro acetic acid (50%, 50 mL) in
all the wells for 1 h at 4 degC. The plates were washed with distilled
water and allowed to air-dry, followed by staining with SRB dye
(50 mL of 0.4% in 1% acetic acid) for 30 min at room temperature. Unbound dye was removed with acetic acid (1%, v/v). The plates were air
dried and solubilized the bound dye by adding 100 mL of 10 mM Tris
buffer (pH 10.4) to each well. The absorbance was recorded at 540 nm
on microplate reader (BioTek Synergy HT) and IC50 values were obtained by plotting optical density versus concentration.

2.4.6. Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC)
Thermal analysis was performed for TPD, PLGA, physical mixture of
TPD and PLGA, and TPD-PLGA nanoparticles using DSC (Mettler-Toledo
International Inc., Switzerland). Two milligram of each sample was kept
into aluminum crucible and sealed with a standard aluminum lid. The
sample was flushed with nitrogen at a rate of 30 mL/min under an
inert atmosphere and analyzed over a temperature range of 35-300 degC.
The temperature was increased at a rate of 10 degC/min.

2.5.4. Assessment of combination effects
The combined effect of two drugs was analyzed to know the nature of the drug interaction from the median effect principle given
by Chou and Talalay , by using the CalcuSyn software (Version
2.11, Biosoft). The IC50 values obtained from single drug treatment
cell growth inhibition assays were used to design the combination
experiments. The cells were treated with cisplatin, TPD and TPDPLGA-NPs alone or in combination (cisplatin:TPD-PLGA-NPs or
cisplatin:TPD) at a fixed molar ratio of 2:1 (cisplatin:TPD). The combination index (CI) values for the selected combinations treatment
were determined using the equation: CI = (D)1 / (Dx)1 + (D)2 /
(Dx)2, where (D)1 and (D)2 are the concentrations of individual
drug in the combination resulting in x% growth inhibition, and
(Dx)1 and (Dx)2 are the concentrations of individual drugs alone to
elicit the same effect . The CI values for drug interaction were
plotted as a function of fraction affected (Fa). CI values of b1, =1
and N1 demonstrate synergism, additive effect and antagonism respectively. Further, we evaluated the dose reduction index (DRI) to
measure the dose reduction of individual drug in a synergistic combination at a particular effect level compared with the doses of single
drug alone .

3.7. In vitro growth inhibition study

The growth inhibitory effect of cisplatin, TPD and TPD-PLGA-NPs

3.4. Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR)

3.1. Optimization of TPD loaded PLGA nanoparticles

FTIR spectroscopy was performed to characterize the interaction between the drug and polymer [Fig. 4]. A characteristic band at 3390 cm-1

is attributed to\\OH groups stretching vibration in TPD. In the spectra
of pure PLGA, the peaks at 1760 cm-1 is attributed to the absorbance
of \\C_O in PLGA matrix. Further, the physical mixture and TPDPLGA nanoparticles showed mild peak at 3390 cm-1 due to \\OH
stretching of TPD and prominent peak at 1760 cm-1 owing to the absorbance of carbonyl group of PLGA. Additionally, the FTIR spectrum of formulation showed mild peaks at 1569 cm-1 which may be attributed to
the stretching vibrations of\\C_O groups attached to hydroxyl groups
via hydrogen bonds of residual polyvinyl alcohol that was used as surfactant during nanoparticle fabrication process . Overall, the results
indicated the absence of any drug polymer interactions.

In order to optimize the amount of TPD incorporated into PLGA
nanoparticles, polymer to drug ratios e.g. 35:1.16 (E1), 35:1.4 (E2),
35:1.75 (E3), 35:2.33 (E4) were evaluated [Fig. 2]. The TPD loaded
PLGA nanoparticles were fabricated by emulsion solvent evaporation
method and drug loading was determined. It was observed that a maximum drug loading (51 mg/mg) was obtained for the E3 formulation
which was significantly (p b 0.05) high as compared to E2 formulation.
Further increase in drug to polymer ratio does not increase the drug
loading significantly. Therefore further experiments were performed
using E3 formulation.

3.5. Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC)

In order to investigate the thermal behavior of TPD-PLGA nanoparticles, DSC was conducted for free TPD, PLGA, physical mixture of TPD and
PLGA, and TPD-PLGA nanoparticles. The existence of an endothermic
peak at 186 degC in the thermogram of pure TPD and physical mixture
characterizes the melting of drug [Fig. 5]. The disappearance of melting
endothermic peak of the drug in the nanoparticles formulation indicated
the presence of drug in molecular dispersion form within the polymeric
system. The pure PLGA and nanoparticles showed a peak at 50 degC,
indicating characteristic glass transition temperature of the polymer.

The optimized TPD-PLGA nanoparticles were readily re-dispersible
in aqueous media and exhibited average size, polydispersity index and
zeta potential of 152.56 +- 3.01 nm, 0.168 +- 0.01 and -17.36 +-
0.37 mV respectively [Fig. 3A, B]. The TPD-PLGA nanoparticles showed
spherical shape under the TEM [Fig. 3C]. The particle size determination
by TEM was in accordance to the size as measured using photon correlation spectroscopy.

Considering the physicochemical characteristics of TPD, a nano-formulation was developed and characterized for various parameters. The
combination of cisplatin with TPD nano-formulation result in a synergistic effect coupled with the reduction in the dose of cisplatin. Further,
the combination of cisplatin with TPD-PLGA-NPs was superior as compared to the combination of cisplatin with TPD. The findings are of considerable significance keeping in view the dose-depending toxicities of
