2.2.1. Preparation of lifitegrast-loaded nanoparticles (LEP)
LEP were formulated by means of varying weight proportions of
concentrations of Eudragit RS100 and PLGA (1:1, 1:2, and 1:3 w/w)
using the quasi-emulsion solvent diffusion technique. To identify
appropriate concentration for the preparation of lifitegrast-loaded
Eudragit RS100 nanoparticles (EUD), 100 ppm drug and varying con
centrations (25, 50 and 75 mg) of Eudragit RS100 (EUD) were dissolved
in 6 mL DMSO and then injected into 1 % PVA solution under constant
stirring speed of 500 rpm and subjected to high-pressure homogeniza
tion at 30,000 psi for 6 cycles (LM20 Microfluidizer, USA). The EUD
nanoparticles were then subjected to 15,000 rpm centrifugation at 4 *C
for 30 min (Beckman Coulter, USA) and collected after washing them
twice with double distilled water (ddw).
Further, for the preparation of nanoparticles with combination of
polymers, lifitegrast (100 ppm) was incorporated in a rounded flask and
liquefied in 6 mL DMSO under continuous stirring till indistinct solution
was obtained. Additionally, different proportions of PLGA with constant
concentration of Eudragit RS100 were liquefied in the above solution
under continuous overnight stirring. The resulting formulation was
injected in 40 mL solution (1%w/v) consisting of PVA as a stabilizing
agent under continuous stirring. This sample was then subjected to high-
pressure homogenization and the final nanosuspension was centrifuged
at 15,000 rpm at 4 *C for 30 min (Beckman Coulter, USA). The nano
particles were washed with ddw for removal of surface PVA and excess
DMSO.

2.2.2. Preparation of hyaluronic-based in-situ drops
Gellan gum drops were prepared by varying concentrations of
deacetylated gellan gum where, the formulation was dissolved with HA
(0.1 %), mannitol (4.75% w/v), Polysorbate 80 (4 % w/v), EDTA (0.03
% w/v) and potassium sorbate (0.01 %). Briefly, mannitol, Polysorbate
80 and EDTA were added in 20 mL of ddw under stirring at 500 rpm.
Gellan gum was solubilized in 10 mL ddw and kept undisturbed to hy
drate for 10 min, followed by heating the solution at 80 *C under con
stant stirring until a transparent homogenous solution was obtained.
Both the solutions were mixed to 80 *C and gellan gum solution was
added into mannitol solution at constant stirring (500 rpm). After
obtaining the homogenized solution, the formulation was filtered using
Whatman(r) filter paper. Subsequently, the water loss caused because of
evaporation was adjusted by adding ddw and the formulation was

4.2.3. Osmolality

which falls in the tolerable range of 200-500 mOsm/Kg. Formulations
GA3 and GA4 demonstrated osmolalities of 186.67 +- 4.99 and 194.33

160-354 mOsm/kg. It has been suggested that hypo-osmolar lubricants
may be more effective at lowering tear film osmolarity than iso-osmolar
drops. Hypo-osmolar lubricants is reported to be more effective at
reducing tear film hyperosmolarity over time, as well as symptoms.
