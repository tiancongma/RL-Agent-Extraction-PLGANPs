## Abstract
The blood-brain barrier (BBB) separates blood from brain tissue, making drug delivery to the brain a significant chal
lenge. This study aims to develop and optimize a novel nanoparticle system that may overcome the BBB and have a
potential effect on glioblastoma. Ketorolac tromethamine (KT)-loaded poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid) (PLGA) RG 503-H
nanoparticles were prepared using water/oil/water (W/O/W) emulsification solvent evaporation. KT-loaded nanoparticles
have a particle size of 155.5 +- 2.08 nm, a zeta potential of -12.9 +- 1.23 mV, a polydispersity index of 0.111 +- 0.035, and an
encapsulation efficiency of 53.46%. A significant portion of the drug (> 90%) was released in 6 h and completed within
24 h. When 3% (w/v) mannitol and trehalose were used as cryoprotectants, the nanoparticles remained physically stable.
Additionally, it has been discovered that KT solution is cytotoxic at high doses to Rat Brain Glioblastoma (RG2) cells,
and KT-loaded nanoparticles are more effective than drug solutions at lower doses.

Arachidonic acid is converted into prostaglandins by
rate-restricting enzymes, which are called cyclooxygen
ases (COX). At present, COX has two isozymes: COX-1
and COX-2. While COX-1 is fundamentally produced in
various types of tissue, COX-2 is frequently produced in
inflammatory and multiple cancer cells . Studies
have shown that inflammation is closely related to cancer,
suggesting that eliminating inflammation may be a viable
strategy for preventing and treating cancer [20, 21]. COX-2
inhibitors may provide benefits in combating several can
cers, depending on their antitumor effects, such as celecoxib
and rofecoxib. The long-standing utilization of COX-2
inhibitors might reduce the prevalence and risk of several
cancers. In one study, celecoxib, an anti-inflammatory drug,
has been shown to have an anticancer effect ; in another
study, it was combined with a chemotherapeutic agent and
enhanced its antineoplastic activity . Nonsteroidal antiinflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) are extensively utilized
for diminishing pain and inflammation worldwide. Even
though the structure of the NSAIDs varies, all of them have
antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic efficacy .

Based on the release kinetic parameters in PBS (pH 7.4)
medium, the Weibull model exhibited the biggest R2 and
MSC values, as well as the minimum AIC values, as seen
in Table 2, for both RG 503 and RG 503-H formulations.
However, it should be noted that if only R2 and AIC values
were considered, there would be insignificant differences
between the Peppas-Sahlin and Weibull models of KTloaded RG 503-H formulation. The shape parameter "b" in
the Weibull model is utilized as a criterion to elucidate the
release profile from a polymeric matrix. A value of b <= 0.75
signifies the occurrence of Fickian diffusion, whereas a
value of b greater than 0.75 but less than 1 implies a mix of
Fickian diffusion and controlled release; if b values exceed
1, the drug release exhibits an intricate release profile [4851]. The calculated values of b for the Weibull model were
0.405 and 0.381 for the KT-loaded RG 503 and RG 503-H
nanoparticles, respectively. The release profile is consistent
with Fickian diffusion occurring during matrix erosion [48,
52]. It is crucial to note that a smaller AIC value corresponds
to a reduced amount of information loss. Consequently, the
model exhibited a lower AIC value, which is deemed to pro
vide a better fit to the collected data [53, 54].

The MSC is a variant of the AIC that has been adjusted
to account for the reciprocal relationship between the two.
Additionally, the MSC has undergone a normalization pro
cess to ensure its independence from the scaling of indi
vidual data points. When comparing various models, it is
advisable to select the model with the highest MSC. Hence,
it is relatively straightforward to understand the MSC's
implications concerning the model's adequacy in capturing
the data. In general, an MSC value more prominent than 2
is favorable [34, 55]. The highest MSC value formulations
were KT-loaded RG 503 and RG 503-H nanoparticles; how
ever, values were lower than 2 (1.921).

Furthermore, different cryoprotectants have varying
effects on the physical stability of nanoparticles during the
freeze-drying process. They play a critical role in protecting
the integrity of nanoparticles, preventing aggregation, and
maintaining their functionality. Cryoprotectants help main
tain the function of nanoparticles by preventing aggregation
and preserving shape. Understanding the cryoprotection
mechanism and the impact of different cryoprotectants on
the outcome of freeze-dried nanoparticles is valuable, par

that when mannitol and trehalose are used as cryoprotec
tants, lower changes are obtained in nanoparticle character
ization during the preservation periods, whereas dextran is
not a suitable candidate as a cryoprotectant. This knowledge
could be applied to design more effective and stable lyophi
lized formulations for various applications. These findings
demonstrate that utilizing nanoparticles as a drug delivery
system can enhance the effectiveness of drugs.

All of the RG 503-H nanoparticle formulations' particle
sizes slightly increased after the freeze-drying. Dextran
added KT-loaded nanoparticles demonstrated the highest
Sf/Si ratio and PDI, indicating that the formulation was
not monodisperse after freeze-drying procedure. When
3% (w/v) mannitol and 3% (w/v) trehalose were used, Sf/
Si ratio changes were minimal for the storage conditions.
All formulations exhibited a slightly negative zeta poten
tial, and after the freeze-drying, no significant change was
observed under any storage condition. When mannitol and
trehalose were used as cryoprotectants, lower changes were
observed in nanoparticle characterization, and they could
provide better storage for nanoparticles.

Designed KT-loaded nanoparticle formulations could be
a promising approach for specifically targeting brain tumors.
Besides, it might be considered for combination therapy
with antineoplastic agents. However, further studies, such as
in vitro and in vivo confirmation of the developed nanopar
ticles' ability to cross the BBB, in vivo efficacy, biodistribu
tion, and toxicity, need to be carried out for confirmation.
