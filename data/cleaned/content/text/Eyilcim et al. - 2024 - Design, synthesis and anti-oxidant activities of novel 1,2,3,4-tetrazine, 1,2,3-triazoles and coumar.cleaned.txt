## Abstract
Nitrogen-containing heterocyclic compounds are currently used for a number of pharmaceutical and
agricultural applications because they have biological activities such as antimicrobial, antiviral,
antituberculosis, anticancer, analgesic, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory and antidepressant. 1,2,3,4Tetrazines and 1,2,3-triazoles are examples of high-nitrogen heterocyclic compounds. Coumarins, on
the other hand, are lactones that form a group of oxygenated heterocyclic compounds found in plants.
In this article, two analogs of 1,2,3,4-tetrazine, two analogs of 1,2,3-triazole and five analogs of
coumarin were designed and synthesized. Their chemical structures were characterized by detecting
their FTIR, 1H-NMR, and 13C-NMR (APT) spectra. The antioxidant activities of all synthesized
molecules were compared at a fixed concentration (0.25 mg ml-1) using the 1,1-diphenyl-2picrylhydrazyl (DPPH) method. Molecules 9c and 9e, which showed the highest antioxidant activity,
were loaded into PLGA (poly(lactic-co-glycolic) acid) nanoparticles using the oil in water (o/w) single
emulsion solvent evaporation method as a model study. Synthesized nanoparticles characterized for
particle size, zeta potential, functional groups, morphology, and release properties. Particle size and
zeta potential of 9c/NP were determined as 216.1 +- 8.944 nm and -14.1 +- 2.40 mV, respectively. The
particle size and zeta potential for 9e/NP were measured as 222.0 +- 12.490 nm and -12.4 +- 1.42 mV
respectively. The study results obtained on model nanoparticle systems with elucidated physicochemical properties may have the potential to provide a promising basis for oxidative stress-related diseases
in the future.

2.1. Chemical synthesis
Information about the reactions carried out under inert atmosphere was stated in the general synthesis method
and reaction conditions. Reagents and solvents purchased from Sigma Aldrich or Merck were used without any
further processing. Dry acetone was distilled by boiling with CaH under nitrogen atmosphere and kept in 4 A
molecular sieve. Dry N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF) was prepared by drying on a 4 A molecular sieve

2

overnight, decanting, and then distilled by vacuum distillation. Dry acetone was prepared by distilling after
holding it on a 4 A molecular sieve. Thin layer chromatography (TLC) on silica gel GF 254 was used to monitor
the reaction. The plates were visualized using staining solutions such as KMnO4 and Vanillin under UV light.
Gallenkamp digital thermometer device was used to measure the melting points of solid materials. Perkin Elmer
FT-IR spectrometer was used for FTIR spectra. 1H and 13C NMR spectra of the molecules were determined with
the Bruker Avance III-500 MHz NMR system. Agilent 6890 N/5973 GC/IMSD system was used for mass
spectral studies and high-resolution mass spectra were recorded on Agilent G6530B TOF/Q-TOF Mass
spectrometer.

2.2. Synthesis of bicyclo[2.2.1]hept-8-ene-3-endo,5-endodicarboximide  
Maleimide (7.28 g, 75.0 mmol, 1.0 equiv) was weighed into a single-necked flask and dissolved in diethyl
(0.68 M). Then, freshly distilled cyclopentadiene (7.0 ml, 80.0 mmol, 1.06 equiv) was added dropwise to the
reaction mixture at room temperature and was stirred at room temperature for 2 h. The solid product formed
after 2 h was separated from the reaction medium by filtration and washed with cold diethyl ether. Compound 1
was obtained as a white solid in 98% yield (12.0 g, 73.5 mmol). The obtained substance was used directly in the
next step without purification. Rf = 0.30 (CH2Cl2:MeOH 100:1). mp.: 184.4 degC-188.6 degC. FTIR (ATR):
n = 3150, 2992, 2954, 2879, 1753, 1694, 1403, 1352, 1119 cm-1. GC-MS (EI, 70 eV): m/z = 163 (M+), 91
(C7H8), 66 (cyclopentadiene).

2.3. Synthesis of N-(benzyloxy)-2-bromo-2-methylpropanamide  
O-Benzylhydroxylamine hydrochloride (1.56 g, 10 mmol) and triethylamine (1.01 g, 10 mmol) was added to a
solution of 2-bromo-2-methylpropanoyl bromide (2.3 g, 10 mmol) dissolved in dichloromethane (0.5 M) at
0 degC. After the reaction mixture was stirred at 0 degC overnight, it was quenched with water and extraction was
performed. The organic phase was dried over Na2SO4, filtered, and evaporated under reduced pressure.
Purification was performed with the help of column chromatography (3:1, n-hexane:EtOAc). A white solid was
obtained in 87% yield. Rf = 0.58 (3:1, Hexanes: Ethyl Acetate); mp = 88 degC-92 degC; FTIR (ATR): n = 3192, 3036,
2956, 2890, 1651, 1497, 1469, 1454, 1112, 1031, 1004 cm-1. 1H-NMR (500 MHz, CDCl3): d 9.02 (brs, 1H),
7.37-7.31 (m, 5H), 4.87 (s, 2H), and 1.86 (s, 6H).

2.4. General procedure for 3a-b
The dihaloalkane (1,4-dibromobutane, 1,6-dibromohexane or 1,8-dibromooctane) (4 mmol) was added to a
solution of compound 1 (1 mmol) and potassium carbonate in dry 2-butanone (20 ml) or dry
dimethylformamide (10 ml) under inert atmosphere, and the reaction mixture was refluxed at 155 degC for
4-24 h. The reaction was monitored with the TLC system. The mixture was brought to room temperature and
filtered. The organic solvent was removed under reduced pressure and the crude product was purified by
column chromatography to obtain compounds 3a-b, respectively.

2.5. General procedure for 4a-b
A solution of NaN3 (14.3 g, 0.22 mol) in water (28 ml) was placed in a flask and allowed to boil under reflux. A
solution of Compound 3a-b (0.15 mol) in THF (5.8 ml) was then added dropwise to the reaction medium. The

reaction mixture came to room temperature, the reaction was taken to a separatory funnel and the organic phase
was separated and washed with water (2x10mL). It was dried with MgSO4, and the solvent was evaporated under
vacuum. The substance was used directly in the next reaction.

2.8. General procedure for 7a-c
Resorcinol (1.0 mmol) and b-ketoester (1.0 mmol) were interacted with ultrasonic waves in an ultrasonic bath at
room temperature in 20 ml THF as solvent and catalyst. The progress of the reaction was followed by thin layer
chromatography with a dichloromethane: methanol (20:1) system. It was determined that the reaction was
completed in 5 h. THF was removed in a rotary evaporator. The resulting solid was crystallized from methanol.

2.11.1. Antioxidant activity evaluation of compounds
DPPH, being a stable free radical, has the ability to accept an electron or hydrogen, resulting in its conversion
into a molecule that is both neutral and stable. In DPPH method, the ability of free compounds to donate
hydrogen or electron atoms was evaluated visually according to the amount of color change of the purple
DPPH solution to the yellow solution and spectrophotometrically by measuring the absorbance at 517 nm
. First, an ethanolic solution of DPPH (0.1 mM; 0.5 mg of DPPH in 13 ml of ethanol) was prepared and
its absorbance at 517 nm (to be used as a control) was immediately measured using a UV-Vis

0.25 mg ml-1 were added to the DPPH solution at a ratio of 1:5 (sample:DPPH; v/v) and vortexed. The reaction
solution was incubated in the dark at room temperature for 30 min. After this period, spectrophotometric
measurements of the DPPH solutions treated with the samples were carried out at 517 nm. The antioxidant

2.11.2. Synthesis of nanoparticles
Because they show higher antioxidant activity than others, 9c and 9e loaded nanoparticles were synthesized. The
process of synthesizing 9c or 9e-loaded PLGA NPs was carried out by employing the oil in water (o/w) single
emulsion solvent evaporation technique, as described in the available literature . Briefly, 6 mg of 9c or 9e was
dissolved in 1 ml of DCM (Ridel de Haen) and 20 mg of PLGA was dissolved in 1 ml of DCM. Subsequently, the
two solutions were merged and thoroughly mixed. The resulting organic phase was slowly added to the external
aqueous phase consisting of 4 ml of 3% (w/v) PVA solution. This was performed using a microtip probe
sonicator (Bandelin Sonopuls, Germany) placed in an ice bath, resulting in the formation of a single emulsion.
Then, the oil/water emulsion was mixed with 35 ml of 0.1% (w/v) PVA solution and stirred for 3 h at room
temperature to allow the organic solvent in the organic phase to evaporate. The particles formed were
subsequently gathered through centrifugation (Nuve 800 R) at a speed of 9000 rpm, then washed three times
with ultrapure water to efficiently eliminate any PVA and unencapsulated compounds. Following this, the

7

particles were freeze-dried and stored at a temperature of -20 degC until further analysis. Empty PLGA NPs
(Empty NP) were produced using identical procedures without including 9c or 9e.

2.11.4. In vitro release study
The 9c or 9e release from NPs in vitro was studied using the dissolution method . During the release study,
lyophilized 9c/NP or 9e/NP were resuspended in PBS (pH 7.4, 0.01 M) to mimic physiological pH, with final
concentrations of molecules set to 0.5 mg ml-1 based on loading capacity. The release study was also repeated in
PBS buffer containing 1% (v/v) Tween 20 (used as surfactant). The suspension obtained was then subjected to
incubation in a shaker at 37 degC and 150 rpm. The suspensions were subjected to centrifugation at 9,000 rpm for
20 min at different predetermined time intervals (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 h; 1, 2, 3, 4, 7, 11 days). Following centrifugation,
the supernatant was meticulously collected, while the pellet was subsequently resuspended in PBS. This process
was repeated until the release of compounds from NPs was completed. The amount of 9c or 9e in the
supernatant was determined by measuring the absorbance at 330 nm for 9c and 321 nm for 9e using a UV-vis
spectrophotometer and then referring to a previously prepared calibration curve.

The in vitro release of 9c or 9e from nanoparticles has been shown to follow a biphasic release profile with a

for 9e/NPs, similar to 9c/NPs. The burst release percentage in 9c/NPs was determined to be 50.58%, whereas in
9e/NPs it was found to be 53.38%. In the second phase of the release of 9c from 9c/NPs, an approximately
constant and low level of 9c release was observed, and at the end of 48 h, the cumulative release for 9c/NP
reached 80.52%. In the second phase of the release of 9e from 9e/NPs, a constant and low level of 9e release was
observed and at the end of 48 h, the cumulative release for 9e/NP reached 69.41%. The hydrophobic properties
of PLGA impede the diffusion of the 9c or 9e molecules to release medium, resulting in a limited, drug diffusion
and release, from the nanoparticles system . It is clear that adding Tween 20 to the release medium
accelerates the release of compounds from nanoparticles. The reason for this is that the affinity of hydrophobic

molecules to the release medium increases due to the increase in the hydrophobicity of the release medium with
a surfactant such as Tween 20 added to the release medium.

## Conclusion
As a result, the synthesis of bicyclic heterocyclic compounds containing nitrogen atoms such as tetrazine,
triazole, and coumarin has been reported. The antioxidant activities of these compounds were compared at a
constant concentration using the DPPH method, and nanoparticle encapsulation was carried out for molecules
with high activity. The results obtained indicate that among the nine synthesized coumarin derivatives,
compounds 9c and 9e exhibit the highest antioxidant activity due to their ability to donate H radicals.
Furthermore, the resonance stability in molecule 9e facilitates easier breakdown of the allylic radical hydrogen,
enhancing its antioxidant activity by increasing its propensity to donate hydrogen radicals. Therefore, the
findings of this study suggest the development of a promising platform for therapeutic applications related to
oxidative stress and may contribute to future research in this area.
