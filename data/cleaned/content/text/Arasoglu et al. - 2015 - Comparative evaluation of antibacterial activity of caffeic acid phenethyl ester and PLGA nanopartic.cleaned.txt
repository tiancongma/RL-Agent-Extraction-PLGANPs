## Abstract
The aim of the present study was to evaluate the antimicrobial activity of nanoparticle and free
formulations of the CAPE compound using different methods and comparing the results in the
literature for the first time. In parallel with this purpose, encapsulation of CAPE with the PLGA
nanoparticle system (CAPE-PLGA-NPs) and characterization of nanoparticles were carried out.
Afterwards, antimicrobial activity of free CAPE and CAPE-PLGA-NPs was determined using
agar well diffusion, disk diffusion, broth microdilution and reduction percentage methods. P.
aeroginosa, E. coli, S. aureus and methicillin-resistant S. aureus (MRSA) were chosen as model
bacteria since they have different cell wall structures. CAPE-PLGA-NPs within the range of
214.0+-8.80 nm particle size and with an encapsulation efficiency of 91.59+-4.97% were
prepared using the oil-in-water (o-w) single-emulsion solvent evaporation method. The
microbiological results indicated that free CAPE did not have any antimicrobial activity in any of
the applied methods whereas CAPE-PLGA-NPs had significant antimicrobial activity in both

Caffeic acid phenethyl ester (CAPE) is a natural bioactive
compound obtained from the honeybee . CAPE has been
reported to have various biological and pharmacological
effects, e.g. antiviral , antioxidant [47, 54], antiallergic
, anti-carcinogenic , anti-inflammatory , immunomodulatory  and antibacterial . There are several
studies in the literature on antimicrobial activity of CAPE on
Enterococcus faecalis, Listeria monocytogenes, Staphylococcus aureus, Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas aeruginosa
and Haemophilus influenzae [26, 27, 51, 58]. These studies
show that RNA, DNA and cellular proteins are possible targets of CAPE [26, 27, 39, 51, 58]. Additionally, TakaisiKikuni and Schilcher suggest that the antimicrobial action of
CAPE is probably based on the inhibition of bacterial RNA
polymerase . Moreover, Lee et al reported that the antimicrobial effect of CAPE is related to outer membrane
damage and reactive oxygen species synthesis in bacteria
. A recent study carried out by Firat et al found that

a crucial role in many biological activities as a therapeutic

on improving the bioavailability of poorly bioavailable drugs

These nanoparticles, exhibiting larger surface area to volume
ratio, have significant effects on pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics of bioactive compounds. Also, nanoparticles
that have very small particle sizes are regarded as ideal

Consequently, it can be concluded that encapsulation of
CAPE in the PLGA nanoparticle system improved its
antimicrobial activity. During the degradation of the PLGA
nanoparticle, pH decreases in the medium/suspension containing bacteria; the hydrophobic nature of CAPE increases,
and CAPE readily penetrates into the lipids of the cell
membrane . Therefore, in vitro antimicrobial activity of
CAPE-PLGA-NPs is better than the free CAPE formulation.
Additionally, the PLGA nanoparticle systems regulate the
dissolution of CAPE, and increase its bioavailability and
bioactivity. Despite the inability of free CAPE to show
antimicrobial activity, when encapsulated in the PLGA
nanoparticlesystem, CAPE inhibits microbial growth, which
indicates that the biocompatible and biodegradable PLGA

nanoparticles could be an alternative to solvents such as
ethanol, methanol or DMSO.
