2.5. Preparation of cisplatin loaded hyaluronic acid conjugated PLGA
nanoparticles

HA sodium salt was dissolved in deionized water, desalted by dialysis using Pur-A-lyzer (MWCO: 3.5 kDa) against hydrochloric acid solution (0.1 N) and subsequently lyophilized. The CP-HA-PLGA-PEG-NPs
were prepared as described earlier . Briefly, desalted HA (1
equivalent) was dissolved in 2-(N-morpholino) ethane sulfonic acid
(MES) buffer (0.01 M, pH 5.5) and then EDC (2 equiv.) and NHS
(2 equiv.) were added to activate carboxylic groups of HA. Subsequently, the mixture was incubated with nanoparticles suspension
under continuous stirring for 2 h at room temperature. The CP-HAPLGA-PEG-NPs were purified by washing using centrifugation at
19,000 rpm for 15 min (Sigma 3-30 K), and lyophilized. The supernatant was collected for the estimation of HA.

2.2. Synthesis of PLGA-PEG-NH2 copolymer

PLGA-PEG-NH2 copolymer was synthesized in two steps by using
carbodiimide chemistry . Firstly, the carboxylic group of PLGA was
activated by the DCC/NHS, followed by conjugation of activated PLGA
with the amine group of PEG bis-amine. In the first step, PLGA
(1 equiv.), DCC (3 equiv.) and NHS (3 equiv.) were added in 6 ml of
anhydrous dichloromethane, and the reaction was carried out for 24 h
with continuous stirring at room temperature under nitrogen environment. The resultant product was filtered to remove by-product dicylohexylurea using 0.45 mm PTFE syringe filter (Millipore). The activated
PLGA was purified and isolated by precipitation with excess cold anhydrous diethylether. The precipitated activated PLGA was centrifuged,
washed to remove unreacted components, dried under vacuum and
stored until use.
In the second step, activated PLGA (1 equiv.) and PEG-NH2

2.7.5. Quantitative cellular uptake analysis
For the quantitative cellular uptake analysis, SKOV-3 cells were
cultivated and seeded into 96-well black plates at 1 x 105 cells/well.
The medium was changed with the C6-PLGA-PEG-NPs and C6-HAPLGA-PEG-NPs suspensions after the cells reached 80% confluence. In
other set of experiment, prior to addition of nanoparticles, HA receptors
were blocked with free HA (5 mg/ml). After the designated time interval (1 or 6 h), the nanoparticles suspension was removed and washed
the cell monolayer with 50 ml of ice-cold PBS (pH 7.4). After that, the
cells were lysed with lysis buffer (0.5% triton-X in 0.2 N NaOH). The
cellular uptake was quantified by measuring the fluorescence intensity
in the desired wells using microplate reader (Infinite M200 PRO,
TECAN, Switzerland) with the excitation wavelength at 450 nm and
emission wavelength 490 nm. The cellular uptake efficiency was represented as the percentage of the fluorescence coupled with testing
sample treated wells versus positive control.

its off-target effects. Therefore, targeted delivery of cisplatin to tumor
tissue could be of great significance. In this investigation, cisplatin
loaded targeted nanoparticles were successfully developed and characterized. The targeted nanoparticles exhibited improved cytotoxicity
in CD44 receptor over-expressing cell line (SKOV-3) as compared to
non-targeted cisplatin nanoparticles. This could be attributed to the
enhanced cellular uptake of the HA-coupled nanoparticles, as confirmed by cellular uptake study. The advantage of site-specific accumulation of the drug in the desired area was translated in the improved
in vivo efficacy of the targeted nanoparticles of cisplatin. Therefore,
looking into the chemotherapeutic importance and adverse effect of
cisplatin, this study, demonstrating the potential of the HA conjugated
nanoparticles in the targeted delivery of the cisplatin, could be seminal.
