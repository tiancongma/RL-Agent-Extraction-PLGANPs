Factorial design formulation optimization and in vitro characterization of curcumin-loaded PLGA nanoparticles for colon delivery
The higher curcumin EE% in that particular study compared to the C-PNPs prepared in the current study can be due to the use of PLGA and PVA of different molecular weights and/or a different preparation technique (i.e. solid-in-oil-in-water solvent evaporation technique). However, other studies that have used the same technique as the current study for preparing PLGA-based nanoparticles for oral delivery of curcumin and another poorly soluble drug glipizide have reported EE%s of 76% and 16–57%, respectively [27,28]. Thus, the EE%s of the C-PNPs in this study can be considered to be sufficiently high for oral delivery.
