Role of nanoparticle size, shape and surface chemistry in oral drug delivery
To increase the residence time of nanoparticles in the GIT, mucoadhesive nanoparticles have been used extensively. Nanoparticles prepared from polymers such as polylactic acid, poly(lactic-co-glycolic acid) (PLGA) or polyacrylic acid have mucoadhesive properties and have been used for oral delivery of docetaxel, tetanus toxoid, cyclosporine, insulin and silk peptide amongst many others [40–45]. Coating of nanoparticles with hydrophilic polymers such as PVA has also been shown to improve both mucoadhesion as well as oral uptake of the loaded drugs [46,47].
