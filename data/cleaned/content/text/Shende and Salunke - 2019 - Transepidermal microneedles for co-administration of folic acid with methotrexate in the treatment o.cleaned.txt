## Abstract
Rheumatoid arthritis, an autoimmune disorder, has increased globally due to dysfunctioning of
rheumatoid factor and anti-citrullinated protein antibody. The clinical manifestation includes
progressive disability of synovial joints due to inflammation of synovial membrane. The outlined
research work was carried out with an aim to prepare microneedle arrays to co-administer folic acid
with methotrexate for transepidermal delivery in the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis. The
microspheres of poly (lactic-co-glycolic acid) containing folic acid were developed by using multiple
emulsion (w/o/w type) system. Polyvinylpyrrolidone-based microneedles arrays of methotrexate
were casted by using silicon based moulds. Microspheres were analysed for particle size, entrapment
efficiency and in-vitro release of folic acid studies. Scanning electron microscopy showed that the
microsphere exhibits sphere-like structure within narrow size range of 7+-1.1 to 13+-1.3 mm.
In-vitro release study showed controlled release of drugs from microneedle arrays up to 24 h.
Pharmacodynamics efficacy study of microneedle array was estimated by using Complete Freund's
Adjuvant (CFA) model in female Albino Wistar rats and showed suppression of paw swelling. It was
concluded that the transepidermal microneedle technology for co-administration of folic acid with
methotrexate for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis is a minimally invasive, cost-effective and an
alternative to the conventional drug delivery system.

4. Fabrication of PVP microneedle of
methotrexate containing folic acid
microspheres

Microneedles containing methotrexate and PLGA
microspheres of folic acid were fabricated by using
polymer PVP. Firstly an aqueous solution of PVP
(1000, 1500 and 2000 mg mL-1) were prepared and
abbreviated as MN1, MN2 and MN3 respectively. To

2

this solution methotrexate (5 mg mL-1) and PLGA
microspheres of folic acid were added and this
dispersion was allowed to be mixed. The dispersion
was also subjected for sonication to free from air
entrapment. About 2 cm2 area of sterile silicon-based
moulds were fabricated using silicon sheet. The
viscous PVP dispersion was poured into the moulds
with special precaution to avoid the entrapment of air.
Then the filled moulds were carefully transferred to
hot air oven for 30 min at 50 degC. The microneedle
array was removed and stored in closed air-tight
container to avoid moisture.

DSC (Mettler Toledo, Switzerland) analyses of folic

10 degC min-1.

4.1.6. Pharmacodynamics study
Female Albino Wistar rats weighing (150-160 g) were
divided in 3 different study groups (n=6 each
group). First group served as a control, second group
received only CFA intradermally while third group
received CFA intradermally and PVP microneedle
arrays on the site of CFA injection. On day 0, the paw
volume of each animals was measured followed by
CFA injection (0.1 ml) in right hind paw except
control group which received paraffin oil (0.1 ml).
After 7 days, microneedle formulation MN2 that
contained microspheres were inserted into the skin
once in a week for 28 days. Paw volume of all animals
was measured by Digital Plethysmometer (IITC, USA)
on day 0th, 7th, 14th, 21st and 28th day .
