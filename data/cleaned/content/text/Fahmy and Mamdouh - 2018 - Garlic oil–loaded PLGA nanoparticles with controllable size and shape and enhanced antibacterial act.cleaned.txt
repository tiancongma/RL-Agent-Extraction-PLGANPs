Nanotechnology plays an important role in targeted drug delivery
systems (TDDSs), and many types of nanocarriers are involved in
the TDDSs, such as carbon nanotubes, fullerenes, liposomes, and
polymer nanocomposites (PNCs). PNCs are composed of a polymeric matrix reinforced with a filler material whose size is on the
nanoscale. They have been widely used in TDDSs particularly
because of the capability of PNCs either to capture the drugs
within their structures (encapsulation) or to bear the drugs on
the polymeric surface structures (surface decoration). This, in
turn, increases the stability of the drugs, as well as extends their
half-lives. More importantly, they will be well targeted to the
organs of interest and increase the stability of volatile drugs,
which will help in the delivery of antibiotics.12,13

surface areas;  improving the hydrophilicity of GO leads to
better delivery of GO to its site of action;46-48 and  the
degree of diffusion of the GO NPs through the bacteria was
higher than for the GO bulk form.49 Furthermore, it is obvious
that there is no remarkable difference in the antibacterial activities of the NPs prepared at homogenization times of 10 and

## Conclusion
Nanoparticle conjugates of PLGA mixed with GO were successfully prepared by the SE/SE method and characterized by SEM
and FTIR. PLGA/GO NPs in the range of 201-319 nm were prepared, and a number of preparation factors, such as solution
settling, filtration through a Buchner funnel, and vortex stirring,
were optimized to create stable and monodisperse NP conjugates. The results reveal that filtration through a Buchner funnel
and biological filters (forming polymeric NFs) has a significant
effect on decreasing the average particle sizes, increasing the stability of the NPs, and enhancing the antibacterial activities
against both gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria. In addition, the results reveal that all prepared polymeric NPs conjugated with GO in this study show stronger antibacterial
activities against both gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria
compared with the original GO. Furthermore, it is obvious that
the formulations prepared at a homogenization time interval of
5 min have better physicochemical properties than those prepared at homogenization time intervals of 10 and 15 min. On
