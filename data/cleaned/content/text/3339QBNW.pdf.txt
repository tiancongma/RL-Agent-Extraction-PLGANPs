ABSTRACT: Chinese yam polysaccharides (CYPs) have received wide
attention for their immunomodulatory activity. Our previous studies had
discovered that the Chinese yam polysaccharide PLGA-stabilized
Pickering emulsion (CYP-PPAS) can serve as an efficient adjuvant to
trigger powerful humoral and cellular immunity. Recently, positively
charged nano-adjuvants are easily taken up by antigen-presenting cells,
potentially resulting in lysosomal escape, the promotion of antigen
cross-presentation, and the induction of CD8 T-cell response. However,
reports on the practical application of cationic Pickering emulsions as
adjuvants are very limited. Considering the economic damage and
public-health risks caused by the H9N2 influenza virus, it is urgent to
develop an effective adjuvant for boosting humoral and cellular
immunity against influenza virus infection. Here, we applied
polyethyleneimine-modified Chinese yam polysaccharide PLGA nanoparticles as particle stabilizers and squalene as the oil core
to fabricate a positively charged nanoparticle-stabilized Pickering emulsion adjuvant system (PEI-CYP-PPAS). The cationic
Pickering emulsion of PEI-CYP-PPAS was utilized as an adjuvant for the H9N2 Avian influenza vaccine, and the adjuvant activity
was compared with the Pickering emulsion of CYP-PPAS and the commercial adjuvant (aluminum adjuvant). The PEI-CYP-PPAS,
with a size of about 1164.66 nm and a ζ potential of 33.23 mV, could increase the H9N2 antigen loading efficiency by 83.99%. After
vaccination with Pickering emulsions based on H9N2 vaccines, PEI-CYP-PPAS generated higher HI titers and stronger IgG
antibodies than CYP-PPAS and Alum and increased the immune organ index of the spleen and bursa of Fabricius without immune
organ injury. Moreover, treatment with PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2 induced CD4+ and CD8+ T-cell activation, a high lymphocyte
proliferation index, and increased cytokine expression of IL-4, IL-6, and IFN-γ. Thus, compared with the CYP-PPAS and aluminum
adjuvant, the cationic nanoparticle-stabilized vaccine delivery system of PEI-CYP-PPAS was an effective adjuvant for H9N2
vaccination to elicit powerful humoral and cellular immune responses.

adjuvant
■INTRODUCTION
Avian influenza viruses (AIVs) have been widespread in
poultry in several countries, which have threatened global
biosecurity.1 As a low-pathogenicity avian influenza (LPAI)
virus in poultry, the H9N2 subtype influenza virus could cause
a decline in egg production or mixed infections with other
pathogens, resulting in a high mortality rate in chickens and
causing enormous economic losses.2,3 In recent years, an
increasing number of human-diagnosed infections with the
H9N2 influenza virus and the interspecies transmission of this
virus have become a potential public threat.4−6 Immunization
is a critical strategy for long-term protection against Avian
influenza viruses. To effectively defend against Avian influenza
virus infection, powerful humoral and cellular immunity must
be established.7,8 The generally used adjuvant aluminum
(Alum) could enhance humoral immune response defense
against most antigens. Besides obvious local and systemic
adverse problems, it stimulates poor cellular immune response
and cytotoxic T lymphocyte response.9−11 It is considerably
important to apply adjuvants to extend the duration of antigens
in vivo and induce humoral cellular immunity to reduce
economic losses caused by the H9N2 influenza virus.

Biodegradable and biocompatible materials and immuno-
modulators derived from natural plants are receiving increasing
attention.12−15 As the main active component extracted from
Rhizoma Dioscorea, Chinese yam polysaccharides functioned as
immunomodulators to improve lymphocyte proliferation and
regulate cytokine and antibody levels.16 It has been reported
that Chinese yam polysaccharides have a molecular weight of
16,619 Da and mainly consist of β-1,3-linked glucose, α-1-
linked galactose, and α-1,6-linked galactose.17 However,
Chinese yam polysaccharides have some disadvantages in
clinical applications including a short half-life.18,19
With the widespread biomedical application of particle-
stabilized emulsion (Pickering emulsion), the adjuvant
potential of Pickering emulsions has received considerable
attention. As a biocompatible and biodegradable biomaterial,
poly (lactic-co-glycolic acid) (PLGA) has been widely
employed in the fabrication of Pickering emulsions.20,21 Due
to the fluidity, flexibility, and high specific surface area of
Pickering emulsions, they contribute to increasing the
interaction with cell membranes.22,23 The emulsion droplet
could cause dynamic recognition by the immune system,
enhance endocytosis of antigen-presenting cells (APCs), and
activate APCs. Subsequently, the immune cells trigger a
powerful humoral and cellular immunity.22,24,25 Recently,
numerous studies have reported that cationic nanoadjuvants
are promising antigen delivery systems.26,27 The positively
charged nanoadjuvants are easily taken up by antigen-
presenting cells (APCs), potentially generating lysosomal
escape to promote antigen cross-presentation and inducing
CD8 T-cell responses.28−30 However, the influence of the
electrical potential on the adjuvant activity of Pickering
emulsions is not known.
Our previous research revealed that Chinese yam poly-
saccharide PLGA-stabilized Pickering emulsions (CYP-PPAS)
provide controlled drug and antigen release properties while
promoting cellular uptake and enhancing the immune
response.31 Herein, we designed a cationic nanoparticle-
stabilized vaccine delivery system of polyethyleneimine-
modified CYP-PLGA nanoparticle-stabilized Pickering emul-
sion (PEI-CYP-PPAS). Meanwhile, we applied this cationic
Pickering emulsion of PEI-CYP-PPAS, CYP-PPAS, and
aluminum adjuvant as adjuvants for the development of
H9N2 Avian influenza vaccines to compare their adjuvant
activities on immune response after vaccination with chickens.
The characteristic of PEI-CYP-PPAS- and CYP-PPAS-based
H9N2 antigen was investigated. After immunization with
adjuvant-based H9N2 vaccines, the effects of various H9N2
vaccines on humoral and cellular immunity were evaluated.
The hemagglutination titers, H9N2-specific IgG levels, and
cytokine concentrations were accessed. In addition, lympho-
cyte proliferation, activation of CD4+ and CD8+ T cells, and
the immune organ index were assessed.
■MATERIALS AND METHODS
Preparation of Pickering Emulsions. The polyethyle-
neimine-modified CYP-PLGA nanoparticles (PEI-CYPPs)
were synthesized by the double emulsion solvent volatilization
assay according to a previous report.32 In brief, a 200 μL CYP
aqueous solution (97.3%, Shanxi Ciyuan Biological Technol-
ogy Co., Ltd) with a concentration of 50 mg/mL was
introduced into 1.8 mL of a 50 mg/mL PLGA acetone solution
(75:25, 15 kDa, Jinan Daigang Biomaterial Co., Ltd), and the
primary emulsion was obtained by sonication at 10%
amplitude for 2 min. The emulsion was subsequently added
dropwise to 10 mL of Pluronic F68 (Yuanye Biomaterial Co.,
Ltd) at the concentration of 0.7% and sonicated at 20%
amplitude for 3 min to prepare a double emulsion. Then, 200
μL of polyethyleneimine solution (PEI, MW: 25 kDa, Sigma-
Aldrich) was mixed dropwise with a double emulsion (WPLGA/
WPEI, 20:1). After stirring by a magnetic stirrer for 3 h to
remove the organic reagents, the PEI-CYPP nanoparticle
emulsion was collected and lyophilized. The Chinese yam
polysaccharide PLGA nanoparticles (CYPPs) were prepared
by a similar procedure without PEI. Our previous study
reported the particle size and ζ potential of PEI-CYPP to be
236.6 nm and +41.5 mV, while the CYPP had a particle size of
225.0 nm and a ζ potential of −20.7 mV.32
The polyethyleneimine-modified Chinese yam polysacchar-
ide PLGA-stabilized Pickering emulsion (PEI-CYP-PPAS) was
made by sonication as in our previous study.33 Briefly, PEI-
CYPP nanoparticles (PLGA: 5 mg/mL, CYP: 500 μg) were
dissolved in 1.8 mL of water and vortexed for 30 s; then, 200
μL of squalene (Macklin Biomaterial Co., Ltd.) was added and
sonicated at 50% amplitude for 2 min. The CYPP-stabilized
Pickering emulsion (CYP-PPAS) was made in the same
method.
Characterization. The visual appearance was pictured, and
the morphology of PEI-CYP-PPAS was observed by Cyro-
SEM (Hitachi SU8010). The particle size, polydispersity index,
and ζ potential of the Pickering emulsion were determined by
the Nano ZS instrument (Malvern, U.K.). The antigen loading
efficiency of H9N2 was detected by a BCA assay.34 NaCl
solution (2 M) was introduced in the H9N2-loaded PEI-CYP-
PPAS and centrifuged at 12,000 rpm for 30 min to separate

Animals and Ethics Statement. HY-LINE variety brown
chickens (male, one-day-old) were obtained from Te Gei Li
Poultry Farm (Jiangsu, China) and were reared with free water
and commercially formulated feed during all experiments.
Animal studies were performed as per the guidelines of the
Animal Care and Ethics Committee of Nanjing Agricultural
University (No. PTA2019034). When the chickens were 13
days old, maternal antibody values of 2.77 (Log 2) were
detected by the hemagglutination inhibition (HI) method.
Animal Experiment. The inactivated H9N2 antigen (EID
50/mL = 1 × 10−9.75) was supplied by the Animal Health and
Epidemiology Center (Qingdao, China). H9N2 AIV is a
reassortant of the rGX55 virus that contains the HA and NA
genes of the A/Chicken/Guangxi/55/2005 (H9N2) virus and
the internal genes of PR8. The virus was recovered by reverse
genetics. The H9N2 antigen was obtained from the allantoic
fluid of chicken embryos, which were incubated with the
H9N2 virus and then inactivated by mixing with formaldehyde
(0.1%) at 37 °C for 24 h.
A total of 90 chickens (14 days old, 18 chickens per group)
were randomly separated into five groups (PBS, H9N2, Alum/
H9N2, CYP-PPAS/H9N2, and PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2). The
chickens were subcutaneously immunized with 300 μL of
H9N2 vaccine containing diverse adjuvants. After 14 days of
initial vaccination, all chickens received a booster vaccination.

in each group that entirely inhibited hemagglutination and
were expressed as the reciprocal of the logarithm with a base of
2 (n = 6).
The H9N2-specific IgG was assessed by ELISA after
vaccination of chickens. Briefly, 100 μL of the H9N2 primary
antigen solution (1:1000 dilution in carbonate buffer, pH =
9.6) was incubated in 96-well plates overnight at 4 °C. After
washing with 1× PBST, 200 μL of 1% BSA was added for 2 h.
After that, the plates were washed and stored upside down at 4
°C for further tests. Following a PBST wash, the serum (1:100
dilution in PBS) of 100 μL was incubated for 30 min at 37 °C.
Then, the rabbit antichicken IgG/HRP (1:5000 dilution) of
100 μL was incubated for 30 min at 37 °C (Bioss, Beijing).
After washing with PBST, the TMB solution and the
termination solution were added. The absorbance value at
450 nm was recorded by a microplate reader (n = 6).
Cytokine Measurement in Serum. Serum was collected
from the vaccinated chickens, and the cytokines of IL-4, IL-6,
and IFN-γ were examined by ELISA kits (FineTest, Wuhan)
on day 35 after immunization (n = 4).
Lymphocyte Proliferation Assay. On day 35 after
vaccination of the chickens, the lymphocytes were isolated
using the peripheral blood lymphocyte separating medium
(Solarbio, Beijing) as in a previous description.36 Lymphocytes
(2.5 × 105 cells) were incubated and restimulated with the
H9N2 original solution (1:50 complete 1640 medium
dilution) for 48 h (BasalMedia, Shanghai). Then, the
lymphocyte proliferation was evaluated using an MTT assay
(Sigma-Aldrich Co. Ltd) (n = 4).37
Evaluation of the Activation of CD4+ and CD8+ T
Cells. A peripheral blood lymphocyte suspension (5 × 105
cells) was obtained on day 35 after vaccination and
restimulated with the H9N2 antigen for 48 h (n = 4). Then,
cells were stained with antichicken-CD4-PE, antichicken-CD3-
FITC, and antichicken-CD8a-APC antibodies (SouthernBio-
tech). All samples were evaluated by flow cytometry (BD,
Accuri C6).
Assessment of Organ Index. To evaluate the effects of
the H9N2 vaccine on organ index, the body weight, spleens,
thymus, and bursa of Fabricius of chickens were recorded on
day 35 following vaccination, and the immune organ index (g/
kg) was calculated (n = 6).

■DISCUSSION
The avian influenza virus has caused extensive widespread
damage to poultry, and the prevention of this virus is a
worldwide problem. Vaccination is an effective strategy to
control the spread of the avian influenza virus.8 Due to the
short immunization cycle of the avian influenza vaccine and
poor immune cross-protection, it is extremely critical to
strengthen the immunization efficacy by the application of
adjuvants.39,40 Aluminum salt adjuvants are commonly applied
vaccine adjuvants. When it comes to the application of
aluminum adjuvants, there are some concerns involving
inflammatory reactions and pain at the injection site and the
failure to amplify the cellular immune response.10,41 Particle-
stabilized Pickering emulsions could promote interaction with
cell membranes to facilitate antigen uptake and APC
activation, and the APCs provide targeted delivery of the
antigen to lymph nodes. The immune cells further trigger
robust humoral and cellular immune responses.22,42−44 PLGA-
stabilized Pickering emulsions can encapsulate the antigen and
the adjuvant, providing an attractive and effective platform for
drug and vaccine delivery.20 Our previous study found that the
Chinese yam polysaccharide PLGA-stabilized Pickering
emulsion (CYP-PPAS) has controlled release properties
while promoting dendritic cell uptake and serving as a safe
adjuvant to enhance the immune response.31,33 Here, we
compared the adjuvant activity of the cationic nanoparticle-
stabilized PEI-CYP-PPAS Pickering emulsion with the CYP-
PPAS Pickering emulsion and the Alum adjuvant on chickens
immunized with the H9N2 influenza vaccine.
As shown in Figure 1B, the polyethyleneimine-modified
CYP-PLGA-stabilized Pickering emulsion (PEI-CYP-PPAS)
was a white liquid that exhibits a roughly spherical structure
with many PEI-CYPP nanoparticles absorbed on the surface.
The particle size of PEI-CYP-PPAS was about 1164.66 ± 22.52
nm with a positive charge of 33.23 ± 0.273 mV. After loading
with the H9N2 antigen, there was a slight increase in particle
size to 1481.33 ± 54.67 nm and a decrease in the ζ potential to
5.75 ± 0.330 mV. The H9N2 antigen loading efficiency of PEI-
CYP-PPAS was 83.99 ± 0.32%, which was higher than that of
CYP-PPAS (Figure 1C−F).
After vaccination, antibody-mediated immune responses
have a considerable impact on fighting against viral
infections.45 According to the antibody results (Figure
2B,C), the antibody level of H9N2 avian influenza in the
group treated with the PEI-CYP-PPAS-based H9N2 vaccine
was obviously increased compared to that in the group of CYP-
PPAS/H9N2 on days 28 and 35. In addition, on day 35 after
vaccination with PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2, the antibody level
was remarkably elevated compared to the Alum/H9N2 group.
Antibody levels in the PEI-CYP-PPAS group remained high for
35 days. IFN-γ is a multifunctional cytokine produced by Th1
cells, which provides antiviral and regulatory immune system
activity to elicit cellular immune response.46,47 IL-4 and IL-6
are cytokines produced by th2 cells, which induce a strong
humoral immune response. IL-6 is an inflammatory cytokine
that is involved in host defense and immune cell
regulation.48−50 The Alum adjuvant could elicit a strong Th2
immune response.51 As shown in Figure 3A−C, Alum-, CYP-
PPAS-, and PEI-CYP-PPAS-based H9N2 vaccine formulations
could significantly elevate the secretion of IL-4 and IL-6
compared with the free H9N2 group. Moreover, the PEI-CYP-
PPAS induced a higher production of IL-4 and IL-6 cytokines
than the CYP-PPAS group. In terms of the results of the IFN-γ
assay, the highest concentration of IFN-γ was induced after
treatment with PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2. Therefore, PEI-CYP-
PPAS could promote both Th1- and Th2-type immune
responses.
CD3 is a marker to recognize T lymphocytes, and mature T
cells have two subsets of CD4 and CD8 T cells.52 Cellular
immune responses and immune regulation are mediated by
activated CD4+ and CD8+ T cells, which are essential for
defense against influenza.53,54 As the main effector cells, CD8+
T cells regulate and kill cells infected by the influenza virus to
enhance cellular immune response. While CD4+ cells differ-
entiate into T helper type 1 (Th1) and T helper type 2 (Th2)
cells, they regulate cytokine secretion and modulate adaptive
immunity.45,52,55 To evaluate the presence of the cellular
immune response after immunization with H9N2 vaccine in
chickens, the percentage of CD4+CD3+ and CD8+CD3+ T cells
and the proliferation of lymphocytes were measured. The PEI-
CYP-PPAS-based H9N2 group could effectively activate the
CD4+ and CD8+ T cells and enhance the proliferation of
lymphocytes compared to those with other vaccine formula-
tion groups. After immunization with CYP-PPAS/H9N2, the
percentage of CD4+ and CD8+ T cells was also more elevated
compared to that in the Alum/H9N2 and free H9N2 groups.
However, the activation of CD4+ and CD8+ T cells induced by
the CYP-PPAS/H9N2 group was markedly lower than that of
the PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2 group (Figure 4). This may be
attributed to the fact that the positively charged Pickering
emulsions with high H9N2 antigen loading efficiency are easily
taken up by antigen-presenting cells (APCs), potentially
causing lysosomal escape to promote antigen cross-presenta-
tion, and inducing CD8 T-cell responses.28−30 Thus, the
cellular immunity of PEI-CYP-PPAS was obviously stronger
than the CYP-PPAS group, demonstrating that the PEI-CYP-
PPAS as an H9N2 vaccine adjuvant facilitated the cellular
immune response against influenza virus infection.
Immunofluorescence images of spleen sections (Figure 5)
demonstrated that PEI-CYP-PPAS increased the number of
CD4 and CD8 T cells, which is consistent with the previous
results in Figure 4. In addition, PEI-CYP-PPAS increased the
immune organ index of spleen and bursa of Fabricius without
no immune organ injuries (Figure 6). Therefore, compared
with CYP-PPAS and the Alum adjuvant, the cationic adjuvant

■CONCLUSIONS
In summary, a cationic nanoparticle-stabilized Pickering
emulsion of PEI-CYP-PPAS and a Pickering emulsion were
utilized as adjuvants to prepare H9N2 vaccines. The PEI-CYP-
PPAS with a positive charge could remarkably increase the
loading efficiency of the H9N2 antigen. After vaccinating
chickens, PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2 elicited higher HI antibody
titers and IgG levels than CYP-PPAS. Moreover, PEI-CYP-
PPAS-based H9N2 significantly enhanced CD4+ and CD8+ T-
cell activation and lymphocyte proliferation and promoted
IFN-γ secretion more than that in Alum and CYP-PPAS-based
H9N2 vaccine formulations. As a result, compared with the
CYP-PPAS Pickering emulsion and the Alum adjuvant, the
novel cationic nanoparticle-stabilized vaccine delivery system
(PEI-CYP-PPAS) was a safer and more effective H9N2 vaccine
adjuvant to elicit powerful humoral and cellular immune
responses. This study introduced a novel concept for the
development of a safe and efficient Avian influenza vaccine
adjuvant.
