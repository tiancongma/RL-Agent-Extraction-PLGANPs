Magnetic hyperthermia studies in magnetite ferrofluids based on bio-friendly oils extracted from Calophyllum inophyllum, Brassica juncea, Ricinus communis and Madhuca longifolia
Studies show that the high heating ability of MNPs is strongly dependent on various parameters like: size and morphology of the MNPs, saturation magnetization, dispersion medium, magnetic anisotropy and external magnetic field parameters like amplitude and frequency [36-39]. MFH have been successfully demonstrated for various types of cancers, like prostate [40], pancreatic tumour [41], glioblastoma [42] and melanoma [43,44]. Further, studies show increased radio-sensitivity in melanoma cancer cells under MFH mediated by superparamagnetic iron oxide MNPs [45].
