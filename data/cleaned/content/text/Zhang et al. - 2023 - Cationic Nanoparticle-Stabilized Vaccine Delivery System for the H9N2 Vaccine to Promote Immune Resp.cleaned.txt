trigger powerful humoral and cellular immunity. Recently, positively
charged nano-adjuvants are easily taken up by antigen-presenting cells,
potentially resulting in lysosomal escape, the promotion of antigen
cross-presentation, and the induction of CD8 T-cell response. However,
reports on the practical application of cationic Pickering emulsions as
adjuvants are very limited. Considering the economic damage and
public-health risks caused by the H9N2 influenza virus, it is urgent to
develop an effective adjuvant for boosting humoral and cellular
immunity against influenza virus infection. Here, we applied
polyethyleneimine-modified Chinese yam polysaccharide PLGA nanoparticles as particle stabilizers and squalene as the oil core
to fabricate a positively charged nanoparticle-stabilized Pickering emulsion adjuvant system (PEI-CYP-PPAS). The cationic
Pickering emulsion of PEI-CYP-PPAS was utilized as an adjuvant for the H9N2 Avian influenza vaccine, and the adjuvant activity
was compared with the Pickering emulsion of CYP-PPAS and the commercial adjuvant (aluminum adjuvant). The PEI-CYP-PPAS,
with a size of about 1164.66 nm and a z potential of 33.23 mV, could increase the H9N2 antigen loading efficiency by 83.99%. After
vaccination with Pickering emulsions based on H9N2 vaccines, PEI-CYP-PPAS generated higher HI titers and stronger IgG
antibodies than CYP-PPAS and Alum and increased the immune organ index of the spleen and bursa of Fabricius without immune
organ injury. Moreover, treatment with PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2 induced CD4+ and CD8+ T-cell activation, a high lymphocyte
proliferation index, and increased cytokine expression of IL-4, IL-6, and IFN-g. Thus, compared with the CYP-PPAS and aluminum
adjuvant, the cationic nanoparticle-stabilized vaccine delivery system of PEI-CYP-PPAS was an effective adjuvant for H9N2
vaccination to elicit powerful humoral and cellular immune responses.

Animal Experiment. The inactivated H9N2 antigen (EID

genetics. The H9N2 antigen was obtained from the allantoic

CD3 is a marker to recognize T lymphocytes, and mature T
cells have two subsets of CD4 and CD8 T cells.52 Cellular
immune responses and immune regulation are mediated by
activated CD4+ and CD8+ T cells, which are essential for
defense against influenza.53,54 As the main effector cells, CD8+
T cells regulate and kill cells infected by the influenza virus to
enhance cellular immune response. While CD4+ cells differentiate into T helper type 1 (Th1) and T helper type 2 (Th2)
cells, they regulate cytokine secretion and modulate adaptive
immunity.45,52,55 To evaluate the presence of the cellular
immune response after immunization with H9N2 vaccine in
chickens, the percentage of CD4+CD3+ and CD8+CD3+ T cells
and the proliferation of lymphocytes were measured. The PEICYP-PPAS-based H9N2 group could effectively activate the
CD4+ and CD8+ T cells and enhance the proliferation of
lymphocytes compared to those with other vaccine formulation groups. After immunization with CYP-PPAS/H9N2, the
percentage of CD4+ and CD8+ T cells was also more elevated
compared to that in the Alum/H9N2 and free H9N2 groups.
However, the activation of CD4+ and CD8+ T cells induced by
the CYP-PPAS/H9N2 group was markedly lower than that of
the PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2 group (Figure 4). This may be
attributed to the fact that the positively charged Pickering
emulsions with high H9N2 antigen loading efficiency are easily
taken up by antigen-presenting cells (APCs), potentially
causing lysosomal escape to promote antigen cross-presentation, and inducing CD8 T-cell responses.28-30 Thus, the
cellular immunity of PEI-CYP-PPAS was obviously stronger
than the CYP-PPAS group, demonstrating that the PEI-CYPPPAS as an H9N2 vaccine adjuvant facilitated the cellular
immune response against influenza virus infection.

The avian influenza virus has caused extensive widespread
damage to poultry, and the prevention of this virus is a
worldwide problem. Vaccination is an effective strategy to
control the spread of the avian influenza virus.8 Due to the
short immunization cycle of the avian influenza vaccine and
poor immune cross-protection, it is extremely critical to
strengthen the immunization efficacy by the application of
adjuvants.39,40 Aluminum salt adjuvants are commonly applied
vaccine adjuvants. When it comes to the application of
aluminum adjuvants, there are some concerns involving
inflammatory reactions and pain at the injection site and the
failure to amplify the cellular immune response.10,41 Particlestabilized Pickering emulsions could promote interaction with
cell membranes to facilitate antigen uptake and APC
activation, and the APCs provide targeted delivery of the
antigen to lymph nodes. The immune cells further trigger
robust humoral and cellular immune responses.22,42-44 PLGAstabilized Pickering emulsions can encapsulate the antigen and
the adjuvant, providing an attractive and effective platform for
drug and vaccine delivery.20 Our previous study found that the
Chinese yam polysaccharide PLGA-stabilized Pickering
emulsion (CYP-PPAS) has controlled release properties
while promoting dendritic cell uptake and serving as a safe
adjuvant to enhance the immune response.31,33 Here, we
compared the adjuvant activity of the cationic nanoparticlestabilized PEI-CYP-PPAS Pickering emulsion with the CYPPPAS Pickering emulsion and the Alum adjuvant on chickens
immunized with the H9N2 influenza vaccine.
As shown in Figure 1B, the polyethyleneimine-modified
CYP-PLGA-stabilized Pickering emulsion (PEI-CYP-PPAS)
was a white liquid that exhibits a roughly spherical structure
with many PEI-CYPP nanoparticles absorbed on the surface.
The particle size of PEI-CYP-PPAS was about 1164.66 +- 22.52
nm with a positive charge of 33.23 +- 0.273 mV. After loading
with the H9N2 antigen, there was a slight increase in particle
size to 1481.33 +- 54.67 nm and a decrease in the z potential to
5.75 +- 0.330 mV. The H9N2 antigen loading efficiency of PEICYP-PPAS was 83.99 +- 0.32%, which was higher than that of
CYP-PPAS (Figure 1C-F).

## Conclusion
In summary, a cationic nanoparticle-stabilized Pickering
emulsion of PEI-CYP-PPAS and a Pickering emulsion were
utilized as adjuvants to prepare H9N2 vaccines. The PEI-CYPPPAS with a positive charge could remarkably increase the
loading efficiency of the H9N2 antigen. After vaccinating
chickens, PEI-CYP-PPAS/H9N2 elicited higher HI antibody
titers and IgG levels than CYP-PPAS. Moreover, PEI-CYPPPAS-based H9N2 significantly enhanced CD4+ and CD8+ Tcell activation and lymphocyte proliferation and promoted
